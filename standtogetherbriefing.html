Power of Purpose

What it is: Research initiative measuring how purpose drives employee engagement, retention, and performance. Created two new indices: Life Purpose Index and Work Purpose Index. Co-branded report launched at the 2025 Human Potential Summit.

Be The People

Year 1 of 5 Survey In Field

Core Concept & Philosophy

The big idea: Develop a way to understand and track the "American spirit" — built around three principles from the Declaration of Independence:

Life → Dignity of every person
Liberty → Freedom to experiment, succeed, and fail
Pursuit of Happiness → Positive-sum view of life; success through helping others succeed

Central construct: Contribution Mindset — approaching problems with agency ("I can be part of the solution"), rejecting blame and zero-sum thinking, believing progress comes from helping others succeed. Brian Hooks stressed that contribution mindset depends on belief in dignity, liberty, and shared success.

Two critical sub-constructs:

Agency: Belief that one's actions matter and problems can be solved.
Belonging: Feeling part of something larger than oneself.

Theory of change: Accurate measurement → corrected perceptions → permission to act → gradual cultural shift. The strategy relies on social proof — showing people that others like them already believe and act on these principles. This is not a persuasion campaign.

Anchor signal: Brian identified a Pew finding as the most useful current indicator — only 47% of Americans believe Americans can solve their own problems.

Guardrails & Sensitivities

Non-political effort. Party ID analysis will be conducted, but publishing requires additional discussion. The story should not be built around partisan divides. The focus is on cross-community insights and shared values — "from-to" mindset rather than within-group comparisons.

Income is a means, not the end. Economic mobility alone is an insufficient measure of the American Dream. Meaning and purpose exist even under hardship (Brian referenced Viktor Frankl).

Voting is a poor proxy. Better behavioral signals include PTA involvement, neighborhood activities, helping neighbors — the "do one small thing" framing.

GPSS Vault Questions — America 250 Angle

Two historical Gallup items will be fielded on the May GPSS survey (~1,000 U.S. adults, phone). These create an early data release timed to July 4th and America's 250th birthday. Strong media hook.

"Overall, do you think the signers of the Declaration of Independence would be pleased or disappointed by the way the United States has turned out?"
Historical trend (% pleased / % disappointed):
1999: 44% pleased / 55% disappointed
2001: 54% pleased / 42% disappointed (high-water mark)
2003: 50% / 48%
2013: 27% pleased / 71% disappointed (most recent)

First asked 2013 in this form. Consistent downward trend in "pleased" since 2001.
"Looking at America in our bicentennial year, how much do you think we have succeeded over these 200 years in achieving the ideals for which this country was founded, as you understand them?"
Origin: Gallup, 1976 (bicentennial). Will be updated for 250 years.
Historical data from 1976 not publicly available in Gallup's online archive. The revival of this item after 50 years is itself a powerful narrative.

Caution

Results may be largely negative or highly partisan. The team has flagged this internally. Both items carry media value regardless of direction — if there's appetite for regulation, it's a story about how far trust in ideals has fallen; if not, it tells a different but equally compelling story.

Power of Purpose

Year 1 Complete Year 2 Proposed

Headline Findings to Know

Only 17% of employed Americans strongly agree they have a strong sense of life purpose.

Those with strong life purpose are 3.8x more likely to be engaged at work vs. those with low purpose.

3.2x more likely to be thriving in life overall.

67% of low-purpose employees are job hunting vs. 43% of high-purpose — a 24-point gap.

Just 22% of employees say work and personal purpose are fully aligned.

Only 30% of hiring managers say personal purpose is very important when evaluating new hires — despite its proven impact.

Three strategies from the report: Make work purposeful. Connect work to employees' life purpose. Support all paths to purpose.

Year 1 Report Summary

"The Power of Purpose: Why Purpose Matters for Employees and Workplaces" — co-branded Gallup × Stand Together report. Ryan Stowers (SVP) wrote the foreword.

Survey conducted Aug 18–25, 2025, with 4,475 Gallup Panel members. Created the Life Purpose Index (10 items) and Work Purpose Index (3 items).

Top sources of purpose: family (74%), job/work (35%), friends (34%), hobbies/passions (33%), faith/spirituality (32%).

Key insight: purpose is accessible across all credential and career paths. Non-degree holders who are financially stable report life purpose at rates equal to college graduates.
