01 · The Problem

The research is scattered.

The democracy field is active and well-funded. The research it depends on has not kept up.

Polls appear around elections and go quiet in between. They ask different questions in different ways, so results rarely line up or add up to a trend. Good work gets done, but it is spread across dozens of organizations and hard to compare.

The result is that there is no single, trusted place to see what Americans actually think about their democracy, or how it is changing. Practitioners end up assembling a picture from pieces that were never meant to fit together.

02 · What It Is

A permanent home for the work.

The Gallup Center for American Democracy is a permanent, independent research center with one focus: understanding how Americans view their democracy, and tracking how those views change over time.

It is not a one-off study or an advocacy campaign but a standing institution — producing research people can trust and cite, whatever their politics. For Gallup, it is also a new model: a center rather than a single engagement.

Independent
Nonpartisan, so the findings are useful across the field instead of becoming a fight in themselves.
Ongoing
Published on a regular schedule, so the field is not waiting for the next election to know where the public stands.
Responsive
The agenda is set with the field, focused on questions that are not already being answered elsewhere.
Practical
Built as a working reference — one consistent method the field can rely on year to year.
03 · The Data

Three kinds of data, used together.

The Center's findings come from combining what usually stays separate.

i.

Gallup's existing data

Years of Gallup measures on trust, institutions, and civic life, so new findings can be compared against a real track record instead of starting from scratch.

ii.

Research from the field

The good work that already exists — academic, government, and organizational — pulled together and made sense of, rather than repeated.

iii.

New primary research

New surveys and interviews aimed squarely at the questions the field cannot yet answer.

07 · Why Gallup

Why Gallup is the right home.

A center like this needs a host that is independent, trusted across the political spectrum, and able to compare today against the past.

Gallup has measured American public opinion since 1935, is nonpartisan by design, and fields research through the Gallup Panel — the largest probability-based panel in the country. It already holds years of trend data on the questions this Center would track, so the work starts with history behind it rather than from a blank page.

That combination — independent, trusted across the spectrum, and deep in history — is exactly what a center like this requires, and what makes Gallup the right place to build it.

The state of American democracy is argued everywhere, and rarely grounded in evidence the whole field can trust. This Center exists to provide it — and to keep providing it, year after year.
