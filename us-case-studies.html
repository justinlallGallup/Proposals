The Gallup Panel
The Largest Probability-Based Research Panel in the United States
More than 100,000 U.S. adults recruited via address-based sampling — ensuring every adult has a known, nonzero probability of selection and results can be generalized to the full U.S. adult population with known margins of error.

Why the Gallup Panel Is Different

Unlike opt-in internet panels, the Gallup Panel is built on probability-based recruitment. Members are selected through address-based sampling (ABS) from the USPS Delivery Sequence File, which covers virtually every residential address in the country. This means Gallup Panel results can be generalized to the full U.S. adult population with known margins of error — the gold standard for survey research.

The panel's robust size enables precise targeting of subpopulations, oversamples of hard-to-reach groups, and rapid deployment of custom surveys. For more than 20 years, Gallup has used the panel to help partners collect high-quality, accurate data on the topics that matter most to their organizations.

Gallup Panel Pulse — Omnibus Surveys

In addition to fully custom studies, Gallup fields the Gallup Panel Pulse — an omnibus survey of U.S. adults that allows organizations to add their own questions to a nationally representative survey on a regular schedule. The Pulse is designed for tracking a small set of items — such as an index, a set of awareness metrics, or emerging attitudes around topics like AI adoption — over time throughout the year, powered by the same probability-based methodology that underpins all Gallup Panel research.

Survey Design

Every instrument is custom-built by Gallup research scientists. Includes cognitive testing, question validation, and cultural adaptation to ensure concepts measure what they are intended to measure.

Data Collection

Web-based data collection through the Gallup Panel, with options for telephone supplementation and address-based sampling to reach populations underrepresented online.

Weighting & Analysis

Multi-stage weighting using iterative proportional fitting (raking) to align sample demographics with the most current Census Bureau estimates. Full subgroup analysis and crosstabulations.

Reporting & Delivery

Comprehensive analytical reports with executive summaries, data visualizations, and full methodological documentation. Raw datasets delivered cleaned, weighted, and documented.

Engagement Approach

Collaborative instrument development with the partner's research team. Includes literature review, item development, cognitive interviews, survey programming, and translation where applicable. Designed for a 10–15 minute respondent experience.

Fielding via the Gallup Panel with real-time quality monitoring. Multi-stage weighting, full demographic and attitudinal crosstabulations, and deep-dive analysis across subgroups. Qualitative research — key informant interviews and in-depth interviews — can run in parallel.

Case Study
West Health × Gallup Center on Healthcare in America
Est. 2018

Since 2018, West Health and Gallup have operated the Center on Healthcare in America — a joint initiative tracking how Americans experience cost, quality, and access in the healthcare system. The Center conducts multiple surveys per year (including an annual Gallup Panel study of ~5,600 adults and a state-representative ABS survey of ~20,000 adults), maintains the Healthcare Affordability and Value Indices, publishes state-by-state rankings, operates a public interactive data dashboard, and drives an active media and policy communications program.

West Health–Gallup findings have been cited in connection with the Inflation Reduction Act, referenced by members of Congress in healthcare cost debates, covered by The Washington Post, The New York Times, CNN, ABC News, Axios, and Harvard Business Review, and shaped the national conversation on healthcare affordability since 2019.

Case Study
Lumina Foundation × Gallup State of Higher Education
Est. 2020

Since 2020, Lumina Foundation and Gallup have produced the State of Higher Education study — an annual survey of more than 14,000 adults across three audience segments (adults without a degree, college graduates, and employers). The partnership produces multiple reports per year (State of Higher Education, Alumni Perspectives, Employer Alignment), each timed for maximum policy relevance, and maintains an interactive research hub on Gallup.com.

Lumina–Gallup data has become the most widely cited annual tracker of public attitudes toward higher education in the United States. Findings on confidence in higher education, the AI-driven shift in student major choices, and the cost-vs.-value gap are regularly covered by Forbes, CNBC, Inside Higher Ed, and the Chronicle of Higher Education, and cited by university presidents and policy organizations nationwide.

Walton Family Foundation × Gallup Voices of Gen Z
Est. 2023

The Walton Family Foundation and Gallup operate one of the largest national research programs tracking Generation Z. Now in its third year, the Voices of Gen Z study fields multiple surveys annually of ~3,800 Gen Zers (ages 13–28) via the Gallup Panel, covering education, mental health, career readiness, AI, civic engagement, and family life. The partnership includes state-level oversamples, a dedicated public-facing insights platform, and topical deep-dives released throughout the year on STEM, rural youth, parenting, and more.

Voices of Gen Z has become one of the most comprehensive generational tracking studies in the country. Findings on student engagement, Gen Z mental health, and the AI-preparedness gap have been covered by CNN, NBC, and Newsweek, and the program's data is used by education policymakers, school leaders, and the Walton Foundation's own grantmaking strategy.

Case Study
Jobs for the Future × Gallup American Job Quality Study
2025

Jobs for the Future (JFF), the Families & Workers Fund, and the W.E. Upjohn Institute for Employment Research commissioned Gallup to design and field the American Job Quality Study — the first nationally representative survey to measure how 18,000+ American workers experience their jobs across five core dimensions of quality. Gallup fielded the survey using the Gallup Panel and address-based sampling, and the full dataset and methodology were released publicly to maximize downstream research and policy impact.

The AJQS finding that only 40% of U.S. workers hold quality jobs established a new national benchmark and was distributed via PR Newswire, covered by Yahoo Finance and industry outlets, and cited by workforce development organizations and the former Commissioner of the Bureau of Labor Statistics. The study's ongoing thematic reports continue to generate coverage and policy attention.
