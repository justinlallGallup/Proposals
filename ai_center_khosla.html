The Prediction

Bold Claims. No Measurement System. That's a Problem — Even If You're Right.

"Starting in about 2030 — 80% of all jobs will be capable of being done by an AI." That prediction has rattled markets, dominated headlines, and reshaped how investors, employers, and policymakers think about the next decade. It may be the single most consequential claim being made about AI's economic impact.

But right now, it's a prediction without a scoreboard. Nobody is independently tracking whether it's happening — how fast, to which workers, in which industries, with what consequences. The data that exists is scattered: one-off academic studies, corporate surveys from companies selling AI, and labor statistics built for a different century.

If you're right, the evidence should show it — quarter by quarter, nationally representative, independent of the companies building AI. And if the transition is more nuanced than 80% replacement — if the reality is more interesting than the headline — the data should show that too. Either way, the person who funds the measurement owns the most credible evidence on the most important economic question of the decade.

The Scoreboard

Your Predictions Create Measurable Hypotheses. Gallup Tracks Them.

Every major claim you've made about AI's impact translates into something Gallup can measure — independently, quarterly, at nationally representative scale. Not to prove you right. To find out what's true.

"80% of all jobs will be capable of being done by an AI"
Fortune 500: Titans & Disruptors, March 2026
What Gallup tracks: The percentage of workers whose jobs have been materially changed by AI — quarter by quarter, by industry, by role type, by income level. Not whether AI can theoretically do the job. Whether it's actually happening to real people.
"If all medical expertise is free… you have unlimited primary care doctors"
Uncapped with Jack Altman, 2025
What Gallup tracks: Whether AI is actually expanding access to health expertise — especially in underserved communities. Are people using AI for health guidance? Do they trust it? Is it reaching the people who can't afford a doctor?
"People will work on things because they want to, not because they need to"
Uncapped with Jack Altman, 2025
What Gallup tracks: Purpose, meaning, and satisfaction at work — measured continuously as AI reshapes roles. Are people feeling more free to do meaningful work? Or are they feeling displaced from work that gave them meaning?
"A child in a remote village could receive better instruction from an AI tutor"
Oneindia, August 2025
What Gallup tracks: Student experience with AI learning tools. Engagement, agency, critical thinking. Is AI expanding educational access — or creating dependency? Tracked through Gallup's Voices of Gen Z panel and extendable globally through the Gallup World Poll.
"This is not an improvement of the old system — it's the end of the old system"
Oneindia, August 2025
What Gallup tracks: The full landscape — are existing economic structures being transformed or merely adjusted? Worker experience, employer behavior, community impact, government effectiveness. The comprehensive picture that isolated studies can't provide.

What Gallup tracks: How workers inside disrupted organizations experience the transition. Are they gaining skills and moving to better opportunities? Or are they being deskilled and stranded? The human story inside the corporate disruption data.

Independent of every AI company. Quarterly. Continuous. Building a longitudinal record of the biggest economic transition in generations.

How It Works

Quarterly Cycle. Continuous Data. Compounding Over Time.

Not a study. A measurement engine. Each quarterly cycle builds on the last, producing richer trend data and a growing public record of what's actually happening.

Phase 01
Assemble the Right People
A working advisory group — engineers, economists, AI founders, workers, small business owners. Not a ceremonial board. People who know what to measure because they're living the transition. The signature partner helps shape who's at the table.
Phase 02
Build the Instrument
Rigorously developed survey questions tested through cognitive interviews with real respondents. Not questions designed to confirm a thesis. Questions designed to find out what's true. Refined until the data holds up to any scrutiny.
Phase 03
Ask Everyone
Nationally representative data via the Gallup Panel — the largest probability-based panel in the country. Not a tech-bubble survey of Bay Area knowledge workers. Address-based sampling that reaches every American — rural, urban, every income level, every industry, every background.
Phase 04
Get the Real Stories
In-depth interviews with respondents whose data tells a story. A factory worker whose job changed. A nurse using AI diagnostics. A small business owner who automated half the work. The human texture behind the national numbers.
Phase 05
Put It in the Open
Quarterly media releases, monthly content, policy briefings, investor sessions. The data goes public. Everyone — investors, employers, policymakers, the press — has to contend with what the numbers actually say.
Phase 06
Track the Trajectory
The cycle repeats quarterly. Trend data accumulates. The prediction either validates or evolves. The record builds — independent, longitudinal, getting sharper every 90 days. By 2030, we'll know.

Gallup has been independently measuring what Americans experience for over 80 years. No AI product to sell. No thesis to protect. No portfolio companies to validate. Just data — nationally representative, scientifically rigorous, and trusted because it's independent.
