01 — The Thesis

The Superagency Argument Needs a Superagency Scoreboard

You've argued that AI doesn't have to rob people of their autonomy or sense of purpose — that it can broaden human agency, amplify what individuals are capable of, and reshape how work gets done for the better. You've called it the "cognitive industrial revolution."

The problem is that the public isn't buying it yet. And the organizations deploying AI don't have the evidence to prove it's working. 89% of executives report no measurable AI effect on labor productivity. Only 12% of employees say AI has transformed their work. The superagency thesis needs a measurement system — not to argue the case, but to show where it's already happening, identify what makes it work, and give every employer a playbook to get there.

02 — The Narrative Problem

You Said It Best: The Public Debate Is Turning Negative Even as AI Gets More Empowering

Every major research organization and think tank is racing to document AI's disruption: job displacement, wage compression, surveillance, bias. That work is important. But it's also the only story being told at scale — and it's drowning out the evidence that the superagency thesis is real.

You've talked about talent leaders having the opportunity to "transform the AI narrative." But transforming a narrative requires evidence, not arguments. The organizations getting AI right — where workers are more engaged, more purposeful, more productive because of AI — exist. They're just invisible, because nobody is finding them, measuring them, or telling their story at nationally representative scale.

The race to document AI's damage is crowded. The race to document AI's human potential — with rigorous, nationally representative evidence — is wide open.

When managers lead AI adoption, employees experience it as empowering. When they don't, employees experience it as threatening. The difference is nearly 9×. That's the superagency thesis in action — and the beginning of an evidence base that could change how every organization deploys AI.

But manager engagement itself has dropped 9 points since 2022. The people most critical to making AI work for humans are disengaging. This is exactly the kind of finding that a comprehensive, ongoing study would surface — and that employers would act on.

04 — The Foundation

Purpose Is the Mechanism. AI Should Amplify It.

Gallup's research has consistently shown that purpose at work is one of the most powerful drivers of engagement, retention, and performance. Employees who believe their work contributes to something important, benefits others, and provides daily meaning show dramatically better outcomes.

The superagency thesis implies that AI should amplify purpose — freeing people from routine tasks so they can spend more time on work that matters. But is that actually happening? Does AI make work feel more meaningful or less? Does it strengthen the connection between a person and their contribution, or does automating the small tasks remove the very texture that gave work meaning?

Nobody is measuring this. It's the most important question for anyone who believes AI can make work better — and it requires exactly the kind of rigorous, longitudinal, nationally representative research that Gallup has been doing for 80 years.

06 — The Study

AI and the Future of Work: Finding the Bright Spots

A comprehensive, nationally representative study designed to answer the question at the heart of the superagency thesis: Where is AI actually making work better for people — and what are those organizations doing differently?

AI & Agency

The core question. Is AI expanding what individuals are capable of — or narrowing it? When people have "a small-to-large team of AI agents facilitating what they're doing," do they feel more empowered or more replaceable? Where is AI genuinely creating superagency?

AI & Engagement

Is AI boosting or eroding engagement? What engagement elements are most sensitive to AI? Does the mode of introduction — top-down vs. manager-supported — determine whether AI feels empowering or threatening?

AI & Purpose

Does AI make work more meaningful or less? When routine tasks are automated, do people feel freed to do higher-value work — or disconnected from the work itself?

AI & the Manager

The critical multiplier. Gallup's 8.7× finding is the starting point. What do the best managers do differently? What do they need? How do we equip every manager to lead AI adoption in a way that amplifies rather than diminishes their teams?

AI & Growth

Is AI expanding skills and career pathways — or narrowing them? Are people learning through AI, or is AI replacing the tasks that taught them? Are skills-first hiring approaches actually working?

AI & Retention

How does AI adoption affect intent to stay? Are organizations losing talent because of how they deploy AI — or retaining it because AI makes the job better?
