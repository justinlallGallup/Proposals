00Context

Why this conversation is timely

NTI is a nonprofit, nonpartisan global security organization founded in 2001 to reduce nuclear, biological, and emerging-technology threats. It marks its 25th anniversary in 2026, under new leadership, at a moment when artificial intelligence is reshaping the questions NTI exists to address.

  • Secretary Wormuth became President and CEO in late 2025; under her, AI runs through NTI's nuclear, biosecurity, and deterrence agendas.
  • Gallup's relevant strengths sit alongside that agenda: measurement of public and allied attitudes toward AI and national security, trended data on perceived threats, and global reach.
  • The meeting is an opportunity to understand Secretary Wormuth's priorities for NTI and to identify where rigorous public-opinion measurement could support them.
01The individual

Secretary Christine E. Wormuth

A career defense and national-security leader rather than a traditional arms-control figure. Her background centers on deterrence, force posture, and homeland threat — the areas where Gallup's AI and national-security research is most directly relevant.

02The organization

NTI and its current priorities

NTI works primarily through high-level convening and policy engagement rather than broad public campaigns. Under Secretary Wormuth, artificial intelligence is a thread running through nearly every active area of work.

Current priorities
Collapse
+
  • AI and deterrence: NTI has published on the benefits and risks of AI in nuclear-weapon systems and warns that AI-driven decision-making raises the risk of escalation and miscalculation.
  • U.S.–China engagement: a leading priority, advanced through bilateral dialogue on nuclear and biosecurity risk reduction.
  • Arms control: with New START having lapsed in early 2026, NTI is pressing to restore limits and to avoid a three-way arms race among the United States, Russia, and China.
  • AI and biosecurity (AIxBio): NTI characterizes the coming period as pivotal and is advocating national standards for DNA-synthesis screening.
  • Regional flashpoints: active engagement on Iran, inspections, and the security of fissile material.
Signature work
Expand
+
  • NTI Nuclear Security Index — produced with Economist Impact since 2012; benchmarks nuclear and radiological security across roughly 175 countries and Taiwan.
  • Global Health Security Index — assesses pandemic preparedness across 195 countries; the current edition is being developed with the Brown University Pandemic Center and Economist Impact.
  • Program areas: Global Nuclear Policy, Nuclear Materials Security, biological policy and programs (NTI | bio), and AI & Tech as a cross-cutting area.
  • Leadership: Sam Nunn is Founding Chair and Ernest Moniz serves as Co-Chair. Adm. Mike Mullen, a board member, led the search for the current CEO.

Where Gallup intersects with NTI

NTI leads the expert agenda on catastrophic risk. Gallup measures what publics, allies, and other societies will support or accept. The clearest alignment is across AI, national security, and deterrence, where Gallup already holds relevant data.

Relevance to NTI
The public balances capability and restraint: Americans want the United States to lead on AI while insisting on safety rules, independent testing, and allied cooperation. That tension is the public-legitimacy dimension of NTI's AI, nuclear, and biosecurity agenda.

Framing the finding
The accurate characterization is not that Americans favor AI weapons, but that support is conditional: caution in the abstract gives way to support when the question is framed around deterrence and adversary capability. This parallels how defense leaders describe the AI competition with China.

A durable measure
Preventing the spread of nuclear weapons has ranked among the top three U.S. foreign-policy priorities in every Gallup measurement. Cyberterrorism and nuclear development by North Korea and Iran consistently lead the critical-threats list. Where NTI relies on periodic polling, Gallup offers durable, trended measurement.

A distinct capability

04Areas of overlap

Areas of overlap

Each area pairs a relevant Gallup capability with a corresponding element of NTI's agenda.

Conditional support for deterrence
+

Gallup's research shows public support for AI-enabled weapons is conditional, rising sharply when the question is framed around deterrence and adversary capability. This bears directly on the public legitimacy of deterrence policy.

AI, biosecurity, and public trust
+

NTI's work on AI and biosecurity — synthesis screening, safeguards, governance — is technical, but the adoption of any safeguard regime depends on public and institutional trust. Gallup can measure where publics support oversight, managed access, and screening.

An attitudinal layer to the indices
+

NTI is known for the Nuclear Security Index and the Global Health Security Index, both expert-built. Gallup's global infrastructure could add a layer of public attitudes — perceived risk, institutional trust, and support for safeguards across the same set of countries.

Building public and political will
+

Translating expert consensus into durable public support is a core area of Gallup's work. The credibility of the messenger also matters on national-security questions, an area in which Secretary Wormuth's background offers particular insight.
