Since 2017, the Georgetown Institute for Women, Peace and Security has produced the WPS Index — the only measure that combines women’s inclusion, justice, and security into a single, comparable score. GIWPS has also extended that framework to the United States, and the U.S. edition became one of the Institute’s most widely used research products, cited by advocates, journalists, and policymakers across the country.

Much of the underlying data is now several years old. Legal protections have shifted, maternal health outcomes have moved, and the landscape of women’s security and economic participation has changed markedly since the Index was last fielded. A refreshed edition — with indicators updated through the most recent available data — would restore the U.S. Index as the definitive, current benchmark on the status of women in America.

Several of the Index’s most important indicators cannot be drawn from administrative records. They depend on high-quality survey measurement — whether women can see a doctor without financial constraint, whether they feel safe walking alone at night, whether they are employed in full-time work. Measuring these reliably, for the nation and for the subgroups that matter, demands a probability-based sample. This is precisely what the Gallup Panel was built to deliver.

“The status of women is the status of democracy. Democracy, peace, and economic stability cannot exist without women’s rights and leadership.”

GIWPS and Gallup propose to partner on the refreshed U.S. WPS Index: Gallup’s national survey infrastructure supplies the citizen-reported indicators at the heart of the Index, GIWPS and the Peace Research Institute Oslo maintain the framework and administrative indicators, and the two institutions bring the findings to the public together. The engagement can begin with a nationally representative national reading and scale all the way to a full state-by-state Index — and, because the same instrument can be re-fielded, it becomes the foundation for a measurement program that tracks the status of women in America for years to come.

Index Framework
Three Dimensions, Thirteen Indicators
The U.S. Index keeps the WPS framework intact — inclusion, justice, and security. Indicators marked below are the citizen-reported measures Gallup is positioned to field on the Panel.
Dimension 01

Inclusion

Women’s economic, social, and political participation — the opportunities available to women to work, learn, and lead in every state.

  • Employment — women in full-time work
  • Working poor — poverty among employed women
  • Education — women with a bachelor’s degree or higher
  • State legislative representation
Dimension 02

Justice

The legal protections women hold and the discrimination they face — from workplace and caregiving rights to attitudes and access to care.

  • Legal protections (summary score of key laws)
  • Discriminatory attitudes — views on women’s roles
  • Maternal mortality
  • Access to reproductive health care
Dimension 03

Security

Women’s safety at home and in their communities — freedom from violence and the confidence to move through daily life without fear.

  • Intimate partner violence
  • Community safety — feeling safe walking alone at night
  • Healthcare affordability — seeing a doctor without financial constraint
  • Gun-related deaths
The Gallup Panel
The Sample Design the Index Deserves
The Index draws on many trusted sources — UN agencies, the Census, CDC, and others. Gallup’s contribution is the set of citizen-reported indicators that require rigorous survey measurement. Those measures are only as credible as the sample beneath them.
What Makes It Different

Probability-Based, Not Opt-In

The Gallup Panel is the largest probability-based panel in the United States. Members are recruited through random sampling rather than online opt-in, which is what makes it possible to produce reliable, projectable estimates — for the nation, for demographic groups, and, at depth, for individual states.

  • + Recruited by random selection, so results generalize to the full population of U.S. women
  • + Reaches respondents by web, phone, and mail — including lower-income and offline women that opt-in panels miss
  • + Address-based sampling (ABS) can be layered in to guarantee coverage in every state
  • + One harmonized instrument, weighted to Census benchmarks, so every estimate is comparable
Why It Matters Here

An Index Is Only as Strong as Its Data

A ranking that shapes policy and press coverage cannot rest on convenience samples. Whether the Index reports the nation or all 51 jurisdictions, the credibility of every number depends on how the sample was drawn and weighted.

  • − Opt-in online panels under-represent harder-to-reach women, biasing estimates
  • − Small, unweighted samples produce unstable rankings that erode an index’s authority
  • − Instruments that vary across groups or states break the comparability an index depends on
  • + A probability panel weighted to national benchmarks addresses each of these directly

Core Data Package

Citizen-Reported Indicators, Weighted & Documented

The survey foundation of the refreshed Index — every Gallup-fielded indicator, weighted and documented for direct integration into the GIWPS framework. Depth of reporting scales with the sample design you choose.

  • National estimates for all Gallup-fielded indicators, weighted to Census benchmarks
  • Breakdowns by key demographic groups — age, race and ethnicity, income, education, and region
  • State-level estimates across all 50 states and D.C. (state-level design)
  • Cleaned, weighted, and fully documented respondent-level dataset
  • Complete crosstabulations and topline data tables
  • Methodological documentation, sample demographics, and margins of error
Optional Co-Development

  • Co-developed analytical narrative and key findings
  • Presentation-ready data visualizations and state comparisons
  • Interactive data experience for exploring results by state and group
  • Gallup.com editorial feature and joint amplification
  • Launch event support and media strategy
  • Executive briefing for GIWPS leadership and partners

Built to Be Repeated

The greatest value of an index is measured over time. By fielding the same harmonized instrument on the Gallup Panel, GIWPS can re-run the U.S. Index in future editions and track how the status of women in each state is changing — establishing a longitudinal record of women’s inclusion, justice, and security that no single snapshot can provide. The 2026 edition sets the modern baseline; each subsequent wave builds its value.

Why Gallup
The Survey Infrastructure Behind the Index
GIWPS already draws on the Gallup World Poll for the global Index’s community safety indicator. For the U.S. edition, Gallup brings the same rigor to state- and race-level measurement at home.

A Trusted Source, Already in the Index

The global WPS Index already relies on Gallup as a data source. For the U.S. edition, the Gallup Panel — the largest probability-based panel in the United States — provides the citizen-reported indicators the Index depends on, measured consistently across all 50 states and the District of Columbia and disaggregated by race. With decades of experience measuring safety, economic participation, health access, and social attitudes, Gallup is positioned to deliver these indicators at the standard of rigor and independence the Index requires — and to re-field them in future editions.
