01 — The Measurement Gap

The Commitment to Beneficial AI Is Growing. The Evidence Base Hasn't Kept Up.

A growing coalition of organizations — including Anthropic and the Gates Foundation — are investing in deploying AI across health, education, and economic mobility. In the U.S., these efforts include portable skills records, AI-powered career guidance, evidence-based tutoring, and tools that link training data to employment outcomes.

The ambition is clear: AI can be deployed in ways that genuinely improve people's lives — not just productivity, but health, learning, and opportunity. But demonstrating that requires something the field is still missing: rigorous, ongoing, nationally representative measurement tracking AI's actual impact on the lived experiences of Americans.

Gallup has been building the infrastructure to do exactly this for over 80 years.

02 — The Moment

American Wellbeing Is Declining. AI Is Arriving. Nobody Is Connecting the Two.

The percentage of Americans considered "thriving" has fallen to 48.9% — the lowest since 2020 and 10 points below the 2021 high. Employee engagement has hit an 11-year low at 31%. Student engagement declines from 80% in elementary school to 40% in high school. Satisfaction with K-12 education is at a record-low 35%. And Americans' confidence in a high-quality life five years from now has fallen to its lowest level since measurement began in 2008.

Into this environment, AI is arriving at scale. But the public conversation is stuck on two extremes: utopian hype and existential fear. What's missing is measurement. Where is AI actually helping? Where is it making things worse? What conditions make the difference?

03 — The Narrative Gap

Everyone Is Studying What's Wrong with AI. Nobody Is Studying What's Right.

61% of Americans believe AI will destroy more jobs than it creates. 46% think it will increase misinformation. 86% expect foreign governments to use AI to attack the U.S. The public narrative is dominated by risk, disruption, and fear — and there's no shortage of research reinforcing it.

But for a partnership built on the premise that AI can be a force for good — in health, in education, in economic opportunity — the absence of positive evidence is itself a barrier to progress. Employers can't adopt what they can't see working. Policymakers can't support what they can't measure. Communities can't trust what nobody has shown to be trustworthy.

The race to document AI's damage is crowded. The race to document AI's potential for human benefit is wide open.

04 — The Initiative

Three Domains. One Measurement System. The Full Picture of AI in American Life.

This initiative would establish the first ongoing, nationally representative research program measuring AI's impact on the lived experiences of Americans — across the three domains where AI's promise is greatest and the evidence gap is widest.

💼
Work & Economic Mobility
Flagship Domain
Is AI making work better or worse for people? How is it reshaping engagement, purpose, retention, hiring, and upward mobility? What separates organizations getting human gains from AI from those getting nothing?
Gallup's foundation: Q12 engagement framework (2.7M teams studied), purpose-at-work research (5.6× engagement lift), 2026 SOGW finding that manager support is the #1 predictor of AI success (8.7×), two years of SCSP public opinion data on AI and work.
  • AI's impact on engagement, purpose, retention, and growth
  • Manager-supported vs. unsupported AI adoption
  • Economic mobility: are workers moving into better roles?
  • Skills portability and career pathways
  • The employer playbook: what works and why
🏥
Health & Wellbeing
Expansion Domain
How is AI changing Americans' experience of healthcare? Is it improving access, trust, and outcomes — or creating new anxieties and inequities? What does AI mean for the patient-provider relationship?
Gallup's foundation: National Health and Well-Being Index (since 2008, ~5,000 adults surveyed continuously across 5 dimensions — career, social, financial, physical, community). West Health-Gallup 50-state healthcare rankings. Gallup World Poll health data across 155+ countries.
  • Trust in AI for health information (vs. doctors — only 48% use medical professionals exclusively)
  • AI and access to care in underserved communities
  • Patient experience with AI-mediated healthcare
  • AI and mental health: support tools vs. new anxieties
  • The provider perspective: how AI is changing clinical work
🎓
Education & Learning
Expansion Domain
Is AI improving learning or undermining it? How are students, teachers, and parents experiencing AI in the classroom? What does AI mean for engagement, critical thinking, and preparation for the future?
Gallup's foundation: Voices of Gen Z panel study (3,793 students, annual). Student Poll heritage (500,000 students, 1,700 schools). Key finding: engagement drops from 80% in elementary to 40% in high school. Fewer than 2 in 10 students strongly agree that what they learn feels important, interesting, or aligned with their talents.
  • Student experience with AI tutoring and learning tools
  • Teacher attitudes: is AI helping or adding burden?
  • AI and student engagement, agency, and critical thinking
  • Parent trust in AI-mediated education
  • Career readiness: is AI helping students prepare for the workforce?

07 — Example Measures

What We Would Ask Across All Three Domains

Illustrative items designed to produce actionable insights — not just attitudes, but evidence that connects AI deployment to human outcomes.

Work × Engagement
"Since AI tools were introduced at my workplace, I feel more/less/equally connected to the work I do every day."
Work × Purpose
"AI has freed me to spend more time on the parts of my job that feel most meaningful."
Work × Mobility
"AI tools at my workplace have helped me develop skills that make me more competitive for better-paying roles."
Work × Manager
"My manager actively encourages our team to experiment with AI tools and learn from the experience."
Health × Access
"I have used an AI tool to get health information or guidance that I would not have otherwise had access to."
Health × Trust
"I would trust a health recommendation from an AI tool if my doctor confirmed it."
Health × Experience
"AI has made my experience navigating the healthcare system easier / more difficult / no different."
Health × Wellbeing
"AI tools I've used for mental health or wellness support have been helpful."
Education × Engagement
"AI tools have made learning more interesting and engaging for me."
Education × Agency
"AI helps me learn at my own pace and in my own way."
Education × Trust (Parent)
"I trust that AI tools used in my child's school are helping them learn, not replacing real teaching."
Cross-Cutting × Trust
"Overall, the AI tools I've encountered in my daily life have made things better / worse / no different."
08 — Scope & Vision

From Study to Standard:
Three Levels of Ambition

This initiative can launch as a focused study or scale into the definitive ongoing measurement system for AI's impact on American life — an AI public good that employers, educators, healthcare leaders, policymakers, and the public turn to first.

The Landmark Study
Foundational · One-Time
The first comprehensive, nationally representative study measuring AI's human impact across work, health, and education — producing the evidence base for beneficial deployment.
  • Nationally representative survey of U.S. adults
  • Three-domain measurement: work/economic mobility, health/wellbeing, education/learning
  • Gallup engagement and purpose measures extended with AI batteries
  • Manager-supported vs. unsupported AI adoption analysis
  • Demographic, industry, income, and geographic cuts
  • Qualitative interviews with workers, patients, students, educators, and managers
  • Flagship report + domain-specific toolkits
  • Long-form narrative content and human stories
  • National media launch + research hub
Quarterly Benchmark
Ongoing · Multi-Year
Transform the study into a quarterly tracking system — the pulse of AI's impact on American life, updated as adoption accelerates.
  • Quarterly fielding with trend tracking across all three domains
  • AI & American Life Index: a composite score tracking AI's human impact
  • Quarterly data releases timed to news cycles
  • Employer, healthcare, and education benchmarking
  • Ongoing qualitative research: rotating panels across all three domains
  • Quarterly content series: articles, data briefs, op-eds, video storytelling
  • Links training and deployment data to human outcomes — the infrastructure the economic mobility pillar needs
  • Annual State of AI in American Life report
  • Advisory council spanning workforce, health, and education
The Center for AI in American Life
Institutional · The Definitive Voice
Establish the go-to center for understanding AI's impact on people — a true AI public good where rigorous research meets constant communication, learning, and action.
  • Dedicated research team and multi-sector advisory board
  • Monthly Gallup.com articles, data features, and op-eds — a constant drip of new findings
  • Regular content releases: infographics, social content, short-form video, press-ready data briefs
  • Ongoing webinar series translating findings into action for employers, educators, and health systems
  • Thematic toolkits: practical, actionable guides for each domain and audience
  • Executive roundtable series convening cross-sector leaders
  • Learning sessions: dedicated support helping organizations act on the research
  • Deep ethnographic and case study research embedded inside organizations
  • Full content studio: documentary-style video, podcast series, editorial partnerships
  • Annual summit bringing together employers, educators, healthcare leaders, and researchers
  • Policy briefs and Congressional testimony pipeline
