Research Framework

Health Is Wealth — More Than Just Financial

This research will produce the first comprehensive, nationally representative dataset connecting women’s health experiences to their power across three interlocking domains. The central thesis: when women are healthy, they are more able to lead, earn, and shape the world around them.

💼

Economic & Workplace Power

How do health conditions — including postnatal complications, menopause, and chronic disease — affect women’s workforce participation, career advancement, and earning potential?

  • Impact of postnatal recovery on return-to-work trajectories
  • Menopause and midcareer attrition
  • Chronic disease burden and labor force attachment
🏛

Political & Civic Power

What is the relationship between women’s health status and their political participation, leadership aspirations, and civic engagement?

  • Health barriers to running for or holding public office
  • Caregiving burden and civic participation
  • Health literacy and political agency
🌎

Social & Community Power

How does health influence women’s community leadership, family decision-making authority, and social capital?

  • Financial health and household decision-making
  • Health-driven isolation and social network erosion
  • Intergenerational transmission of health & power
The Case for Representative Data

Why Data Collection Methodology Matters

For research that aims to influence policy and shape public understanding, how data is collected matters as much as what is asked. The choice between representative and online-only data collection has meaningful implications for the credibility, reach, and impact of the findings.

Representative Data Collection Gallup Recommends

  • Reaches offline, rural, and marginalized populations — the women often most affected by health-power gaps
  • Uses probability-based sampling for statistically valid national estimates
  • Employs face-to-face (CAPI) or telephone (CATI) methods depending on country infrastructure
  • Aligned with UN SDG indicator methodology and international statistical standards
  • Results are directly comparable across countries using consistent Gallup World Poll protocols
  • Findings carry the evidentiary weight needed for policy advocacy and media credibility
Trade-off: Higher investment per country (~$115,000) and longer fielding timelines (12–16 weeks), but produces data with the rigor needed to drive systemic change.

Online-Only Data Collection Alternative

  • Faster deployment with shorter fielding windows (6–8 weeks)
  • Lower cost per country (~$50,000), enabling broader geographic coverage within budget
  • Well-suited for countries with high internet penetration and established online panel infrastructure
  • Effective for directional insights and initial exploration of research questions
  • Ideal for topics where the target population is concentrated among internet users
  • Can be upgraded to representative data collection in future waves
Trade-off: Systematically excludes populations without internet access — often the poorest, most rural, and oldest women — which can skew findings and limit policy applicability, particularly in Sub-Saharan Africa and parts of India.

Gallup’s perspective: For research intended to inform global policy, influence media narratives, and hold up under scrutiny from policymakers and advocates, representative data collection is the gold standard. It is the methodology behind the Gallup World Poll, the Hologic Global Women’s Health Index, and the data that feeds United Nations Sustainable Development Goal indicators. Online-only data serves a valuable complementary role — particularly for rapid-cycle insights or in countries where internet penetration among the target population is high — but should not be the sole basis for flagship findings.

Phased Approach

From Measurement to Movement

The research will unfold in three complementary phases — each building on the last to move from data to narrative to action.

01

Core Quantitative Research

Survey instrument design, data collection across selected countries, weighting, analysis, and a comprehensive research report with findings mapped to the health-power framework.

02

Qualitative & Contextual Research

Key informant interviews with health and policy experts, and in-depth interviews with women to surface the lived experiences behind the data — giving the numbers human context.

03

Communications & Activation

Media partnerships, events, digital content, and an interactive dashboard to ensure findings reach the right audiences — policymakers, advocates, employers, and the public.

In partnership with Hologic, Gallup created the world’s most comprehensive global survey documenting women’s health across 142+ countries. Now in its fifth year, the Index has become a critical accountability tool, with elements integrated into the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation’s Global Gender Gap Report. Policy & Global Governance

Driving Legislation & Institutional Action

Gallup’s ILO Women at Work study — the first-ever global account of attitudes toward women and work across 142 countries — provided data that ILO country teams used in ministerial briefings to advocate for the adoption of Convention 190 against workplace violence and harassment. Gallup’s FINDEX data became the official G-20 Financial Inclusion Indicator and tracks the World Bank’s Universal Financial Access Goal, informing financial inclusion strategies in 110+ countries.
