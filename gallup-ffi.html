Concept Note — Joint Research Initiative

The Future of the Manager in an AI-Driven Workplace

A large-scale, longitudinal research program to fill the data gap on AI’s impact on managers — and to produce actionable toolkits for the leaders and organizations navigating this transition.

AI is transforming every dimension of how organizations operate — but the role most affected, the manager, is also the least studied. This initiative proposes to change that.

The Problem

Leaders Are Making Consequential Decisions Without Evidence

Managers sit at the epicenter of the AI transition. They translate strategy into execution, develop talent, and sustain the culture that makes organizations perform. Gallup’s research has consistently found that managers account for 70% of the variance in team engagement. And yet, manager engagement itself fell from 30% to 27% globally in 2024 — the sharpest decline of any group in the workforce — even as organizations accelerated AI adoption. Gallup’s 2025 State of the Global Workplace report, surveying nearly 250,000 workers across 160 countries, describes this as a pivotal moment: employees, particularly managers, feel disconnected at precisely the time they need to lead through AI-driven change.

The data that exists on AI’s workplace impact is cross-sectional, anecdotal, and concentrated in the technology sector. No one has built a longitudinal, nationally representative dataset tracking how AI adoption is actually changing what managers do — how their responsibilities shift, what skills they need, where organizations should invest, and what happens to the teams they lead. Organizations are restructuring management, deploying AI tools, and redesigning training programs largely without evidence.

This gap is not just a research problem. It is a strategic risk for every organization investing in AI — and for every funder investing in the future of work.

The Solution

A Partnership Built to Fill This Gap

Future Forward Institute and Gallup propose a joint research program that combines the intellectual framework with the research infrastructure to produce evidence at scale — and translate it into tools leaders can deploy immediately.

The Project

What We Will Build Together

The program has two integrated components — a longitudinal research engine and a practitioner toolkit system — connected by a dissemination strategy designed to build a field, not just produce reports.

01

Longitudinal Research on Managers, Employees & Leaders

Research Engine

A probability-based, nationally representative survey fielded annually over five years — creating the first longitudinal dataset tracking how AI adoption changes the manager role in practice. The survey will reach managers at every level (frontline through senior), with an oversample of Chief Learning Officers, stratified by industry, organization size, and region. The same respondents will be tracked year over year, enabling measurement of actual change — not snapshots.

Supplementing the quantitative core, semi-structured interviews and focus groups with managers and CLOs will capture context, resistance patterns, and emerging practice — the stories behind the data.

Quantitative Survey

3,000–5,000 managers per wave via Gallup Panel + address-based sampling. 300–500 CLO oversample. Longitudinal panel tracking. Annual waves over five years.

Core Measures

AI adoption and usage in management tasks. Shifts in time allocation, decision-making authority, and span of control. Manager confidence, capability gaps, and training needs. Employee experience outcomes tied to the Win-Win Workplace™ pillars.

Qualitative Deep Dives

40–60 semi-structured interviews per wave with managers and CLOs. Focus groups organized by industry and management level. Designed for both research rigor and compelling narrative.

Why Longitudinal

Cross-sectional surveys can describe the current moment but cannot track change. Following the same managers over five years reveals how AI adoption actually transforms roles, skills, and team dynamics — the causal patterns that single-wave studies miss.

02

Actionable Toolkits & Thought Leadership

Translation

Each wave of data feeds directly into practitioner toolkits — co-designed with CLOs and senior talent leaders through Future Forward Institute’s practitioner network — that help organizations assess their own readiness, redesign manager development programs, and integrate AI capability alongside human capability. These are diagnostic instruments, planning frameworks, and decision tools designed for CLOs and CHROs to deploy in their own organizations.

Alongside the toolkits, a structured dissemination strategy ensures findings reach the audiences who can act: annual co-authored research reports, Gallup.com feature articles, media-ready data packages, and Summit presentations.

Organizational Readiness Diagnostic

A structured assessment CLOs can deploy to evaluate where their organization stands on AI integration — benchmarked against national data from the survey.

Manager Development Redesign Guide

Evidence-based playbook for retooling manager development for AI-augmented work — capability frameworks, curriculum design, and measurement.

Practitioner Working Sessions

Facilitated sessions with the CLO and CHRO network to test findings, stress-test tools, and build the community of practice that carries this work into organizations.

Thought Leadership & Media

Annual flagship report, Gallup.com articles, executive summaries, infographic series, and content designed for the Win-Win Workplace™ Summit and major industry convenings.

Expected Outcomes

What This Work Will Produce

By the end of this five-year program, this partnership will have produced the first longitudinal evidence base on how AI is transforming the manager role — a dataset that does not exist anywhere today. Organizations, funders, and policymakers will have a rigorous, nationally representative foundation for decisions they are currently making in the dark.

But data alone is not the goal. The toolkit system will give CLOs and CHROs a set of replicable instruments they can deploy immediately — diagnostic assessments benchmarked against national data, development frameworks grounded in evidence, and planning tools that help organizations design AI integration with the manager-employee relationship at the center. A practitioner co-design process ensures these tools are tested and refined by the leaders who will use them.

The dissemination strategy is designed to build a field, not just publish findings. Annual data releases, media placements, and Summit presentations will sustain public attention across all five years. Content tailored for workforce development stakeholders and the broader investment community will help align strategies with evidence as it develops. The aspiration is not a research report that sits on a shelf. It is a sustained, evidence-driven movement that changes how organizations invest in their managers through the AI transition.

Why This Is Critical — And Why Now

The Window for Getting This Right Is Closing

AI adoption in the U.S. workforce is accelerating rapidly. Gallup’s tracking data shows that 45% of U.S. employees were using AI at work by Q3 2025 — and that number is climbing quarter over quarter. At the same time, Gallup’s 2025 State of the Global Workplace report found that manager engagement fell to 27%, with the sharpest declines among young managers and women managers. The people who need to lead the AI transition are themselves struggling.

Gallup’s own research has surfaced a critical finding: despite rising AI investment, few firms are seeing return on that investment. The missing link is managers who can guide employees through adoption. This is already showing up in Gallup’s workplace data. But no one has yet built the longitudinal research program to track this relationship systematically and translate it into action.

The decisions being made right now — about management structures, training investments, AI deployment strategies — will shape the workplace for a generation. The organizations and funders making those decisions deserve an evidence base. This program will build it.

The Framework Behind This Initiative

Dr. Angela Jackson — Harvard researcher and professor, CEO of Future Forward Institute, and author of the New York Times bestseller The Win-Win Workplace — has built the intellectual foundation this program extends into the AI era.

Dr. Jackson’s career spans global operating roles at Viacom and Nokia, a tenure as Managing Partner at New Profit where she architected the Future of Work Grand Challenge — an initiative that reskilled 25,000 workers displaced by COVID-19 and placed them in living-wage jobs — and her work as a Harvard researcher and professor leading studies on how employers can create workplaces where people and businesses thrive together. Her research across more than 1,200 companies, including Walmart, Google, and JPMorgan Chase, produced the first reliable quantification of the financial impact of investing in people. From that research, nine strategies emerged — the Win-Win Workplace™ framework. Published in Harvard Business Review, Stanford Social Innovation Review, Forbes, Fortune, and The Wall Street Journal, and presented at TED, the Economist, and the World Economic Forum, this framework now provides the measurement architecture for this initiative. The Power of This Partnership

Stronger Together: Framework Meets Infrastructure

This partnership brings together two complementary strengths that amplify each other. Future Forward Institute contributes the intellectual framework (the nine Win-Win Workplace™ pillars), a deep practitioner network of CLOs and CHROs, the Harvard research platform, and a powerful amplification infrastructure through the Summit, a bestselling platform, and established media reach. Gallup contributes the research infrastructure to generate evidence at the scale and rigor that framework demands: a probability-based panel of 100,000+ adults, 800+ research professionals, longitudinal tracking systems, the Q12 benchmark, and a strategic communications engine that delivers findings to 30M+ readers annually.

Together, this partnership can produce what neither think-tank reports nor consulting white papers can: nationally representative, longitudinal data that tracks real change over time — translated into replicable tools through a practitioner co-design process that ensures those tools actually work in organizations. The combination of world-class research methodology with a practitioner translation network is what makes this program unique.

From Concept to Coalition

The Path Forward

This is a five-year program with a clear arc from co-design to sustained impact. Each phase builds on the last.

01
Alignment

Research Co-Design

Joint scoping sessions to align on priority industries, regions, and Win-Win Workplace™ pillars for Year 1. Gallup and Future Forward Institute collaborate on a survey instrument that integrates the nine pillars with AI-specific measures — covering adoption, attitudes, capability, and organizational investment. Qualitative protocols are designed in parallel: interview guides for managers and CLOs, focus group structures by industry and management level.

02
Year 1

Nationally Representative Data Collection

Quantitative fielding via Gallup Panel and address-based sampling: 3,000–5,000 managers, 300–500 CLOs, nationally representative and stratified. Simultaneously, 40–60 in-depth qualitative interviews with managers and CLOs across industries — capturing the lived experience of managing alongside AI. Focus groups organized by role level and sector. All data weighted and analyzed with Gallup’s dedicated statisticians.

03
Year 1–2

Analysis, Reporting & Toolkit Development

Findings synthesized into a flagship annual report co-authored by Future Forward Institute and Gallup. Simultaneously, data feeds into the first wave of practitioner toolkits — organizational readiness diagnostics, manager development redesign guides, and AI integration planning frameworks — co-designed with the practitioner network at Harvard. Industry-specific modules developed for priority sectors.

04
Ongoing

Strategic Communications & Activation

Gallup.com feature articles reaching 30M+ readers. Media-ready data packages and targeted pitching to outlets covering AI, management, and the future of work. Launch events with press, corporate leaders, and policymakers. Win-Win Workplace™ Summit presentations. Satellite media tours and social content. Tailored briefings for workforce development stakeholders and investment partners with data formatted for their own communications and decision-making.

05
Ongoing

Employer Engagement & Field-Building

Direct engagement with employers through the CLO and CHRO practitioner network to pilot toolkits, gather implementation feedback, and build case studies of organizations using the tools. Conference placements at SHRM, ATD, Aspen Ideas, and the World Economic Forum. Roundtables with workforce development leaders and employers. The goal is not just to publish research but to build a sustained community of practice around evidence-driven manager investment in the AI era.

06
Years 2–5

Longitudinal Tracking & Iteration

Annual survey waves tracking the same respondents over time — measuring actual change in manager roles, capabilities, and team outcomes as AI adoption deepens. Each year’s data refines and expands the toolkit system. Qualitative deep dives evolve based on emerging patterns. By Year 5, the program will have produced the most comprehensive longitudinal dataset on AI’s impact on the workplace relationship in existence — and a toolkit ecosystem tested across industries and refined over five annual cycles.
