A Research Center Concept — Gallup × Antenna Up · July 2026

The Center for American Men

Gallup — the most trusted name in measurement — and Antenna Up — the platform building the cross-sector ecosystem for men’s health and wellbeing — propose a research center: a core evidence base created and maintained by Gallup, a year-round program of reports and ongoing dissemination, and a standing calendar of convenings and activations that carry the findings into culture, workplaces and policy. This concept sets out the Center, and the founding support that brings it to life.

Across education, work, health and connection, the data on American men has been moving in one direction — and the country has begun to notice. Boys trail girls at nearly every stage of schooling. Men earn a shrinking share of college degrees. Prime-age men participate in the labor force at rates their grandfathers would not recognize. Men die younger, die by suicide at several times the rate of women, and report far fewer close friendships than they did a generation ago. None of this is a claim against anyone else’s progress. Men’s wellbeing and everyone else’s are linked — and right now, the country is flying blind on half the equation.

The attention has arrived: best-selling books, new institutes, state initiatives and rising employer concern have made the condition of American men a national conversation. What has not arrived is the infrastructure beneath it — on either side. The evidence is real but fragmented, hosted in disparate sources, measured on inconsistent instruments, impossible to see whole. And the ecosystem that should carry that evidence into boardrooms, newsrooms and statehouses has, until recently, not existed at all: brands and leaders who want to engage men’s wellbeing have had no safe stage to stand on, and credible messengers have had no credible numbers to carry.

What This Partnership Proposes

Each side of that gap now has an answer — and they belong together. Gallup, founded in 1935 to report the will of the people, brings the science: a core evidence base on American men, built and maintained to Gallup’s standard and grounded in nine decades of trend. Antenna Up, the company platforming the conversations and convenings uplifting the health and wellbeing of men and boys, brings the signal: two national summits, the Buoys Club community, credible male messengers and a proven model for de-risking this space for corporate and philanthropic leaders. The partnership joins them in one institution — the Center for American Men — publishing across the year, convening across the country, and disseminating continuously, so the national conversation about men finally runs on evidence.

The Premise

The national conversation about men has outrun the national evidence on men — and the evidence has no ecosystem built to carry it. Gallup supplies the measurement no one else can field. The Measurement Beneath Everything

Built once, drawn on all year

The Center’s evidence base is a standing measurement program, not a one-off project: recurring waves of 5,000+ American men and women, fielded on the Gallup Panel — the largest probability-based panel in the United States — with a dedicated young men oversample so the group at the center of the national conversation is measured in real depth, not inferred from thin subsamples. The instrument spans five research domains, each built to sustain its own reporting while contributing to the whole.

The Case for Support

Why fund this — and why now

The premise every funder can stand on: men’s wellbeing and everyone else’s are interconnected, not competing — and a contested national conversation needs numbers no one can argue with.

For Foundations

The evidence is the bottleneck

Every organization working on men and boys builds programs, makes grants and testifies without a shared fact base — the field’s single largest structural gap. Funding the open core creates a nonpartisan public good the entire field cites, de-polarizes a contested space, and frames men’s wellbeing the way the evidence does: linked to the wellbeing of women, families and communities.

For Corporate Partners

De-risked entry, exclusive insight

Brands and employers want to engage men’s wellbeing — and fear the misstep. The Center pairs Gallup’s credibility with Antenna Up’s proven brand-safe model: sponsors have reported immediate relationship value with zero negative backlash. Category-exclusive modules add proprietary intelligence on male consumers and workers no competitor holds.

For the Field & the Public

A rhythm, a record, a shared table

The conversation about men currently runs on anecdote — and the information vacuum is being filled by the loudest voices, not the most credible ones. A standing center with a fixed publishing rhythm, a public evidence hub and credible messengers gives the public story a spine — and gives every stakeholder the same numbers to work from.

Why This Pairing

The most trusted numbers in America — and the rooms already built to receive them.

Neither partner can do this alone. Gallup can build the definitive evidence base on American men, but a research firm cannot build a movement’s community, its summits or its messengers. Antenna Up has built exactly those — and proven that brands, funders and leaders will show up for this subject when the stage is credible — but a convening platform cannot manufacture ninety years of measurement authority. Together, the Center holds both halves of what this moment requires: findings that can be quoted by anyone and argued by no one, and a signal strong enough to reach the men themselves.
