Our approach

A study built to be unique — and to be covered

Based on our review of the landscape, Gallup will recommend the topic areas that give OnePay the clearest path to ownership and coverage.

1

Own uncovered ground

Gallup will recommend topic areas that are not covered in other Gallup surveys or in other publicly available research — so the OnePay–Gallup study is truly unique and maximizes media coverage. We design the instrument with these topics in mind.

2

Start where the impact is immediate; build toward an index

Year 1 focuses on the topics that generate the most impact in the immediate term, laying the foundation to build toward a more comprehensive index in later years.

3

Year 1 focus: how Americans navigate their financial lives day to day

For the Year 1 study, Gallup proposes topic areas that fully encompass how Americans are actually navigating their financial lives day to day.

4

Examine real behavior, directly

Rather than attitudes alone, we go straight at what people actually do across the financial ecosystem — the true, lived experience of managing money today.

Year 1 focus areas

  • Multiple platforms used — the real mix of apps, tools, and products people rely on
  • Habits & behaviors — how people actually work with the products in their day-to-day
  • Credit needs — special emphasis on how Americans think about and use credit
  • Where activity consolidates — where people are bringing their financial life together
  • The fragmented wallet — the time, effort, challenges, and barriers of managing money across many apps
  • True experiences — lived behavior across multiple apps, not stated preference
