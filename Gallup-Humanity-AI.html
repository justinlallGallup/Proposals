The Gallup Center for AI & American Life

Gallup is establishing a dedicated research center to give Americans an ongoing, credible, evidence-based voice in how AI is reshaping their democracy, their work, their education, their culture, and their security. We are seeking one signature partner to shape the initiative.

The Moment

AI Is Arriving Faster Than People's Ability to Shape It

AI is transforming how Americans work, learn, consume information, participate in democracy, and experience daily life. These changes are happening at extraordinary speed — and largely without the input of the people most affected by them.

Americans are paying attention. They're concerned. And they feel excluded. Only 2% fully trust AI. A majority believe AI will destroy more jobs than it creates. Nearly half expect it to increase the spread of misinformation. Almost 80% want safety rules, even at the cost of slower development. And yet only a fraction feel they have any voice in how AI is developed and deployed.

The people at the center of this transformation don't yet have the evidence they need to be heard — and the organizations working to ensure AI serves people, not the other way around, don't yet have the measurement infrastructure to know whether that's actually happening.

The Center

Gallup Is Building a Dedicated Research Center. One Partner Will Shape It.

The Gallup Center for AI & American Life will be an ongoing research and public engagement program — the first continuous, nationally representative effort to give Americans a voice in how AI is changing their lives. Not what technologists say AI will do. Not what economists project. What people are actually experiencing — across democracy, work, education, culture, and security.

Gallup is committing its measurement infrastructure, its scientific credibility, and its 80-year track record of giving people a voice on the issues that matter most. We are seeking one signature partner to co-shape the research agenda, the public platform, and the national conversation the Center will drive.

The signature partner doesn't just fund research. They shape what questions get asked, how findings reach the public, and which audiences the Center convenes. They become the name alongside Gallup on the most credible, ongoing measurement of AI's impact on American life.

If Humanity AI Is the Partner

The Center's research agenda would be organized around the coalition's five priority areas — ensuring that Americans have the evidence and the voice to shape AI across every dimension of their lives:

  • How AI is affecting democratic participation, civic trust, and the information environment Americans rely on to govern themselves
  • Whether AI in education is serving all students, expanding access, and strengthening learning — or widening gaps and creating dependency
  • How AI is reshaping cultural production, creative work, and the media ecosystem — and whether artists, creators, and the public have adequate protections
  • Whether AI is enhancing human work or replacing it — and whether workers have agency, voice, and dignity in how AI is deployed
  • How Americans experience AI's role in their security — from national defense to community safety to personal surveillance
  • Ongoing measurement of whether the coalition's investments are translating into real protections and real gains for the people they're designed to serve

Research that sits in a report doesn't protect anyone. The Center is designed to produce a sustained, visible, public platform — a constant presence in the national conversation that ensures people's actual experiences with AI inform the decisions being made about it.

The signature partner's brand is embedded throughout — not as a funder acknowledged in footnotes, but as the co-author of the most credible, most-cited source on how AI is changing American life.

The Research

Five Domains. Continuous Measurement. The People's Evidence.

The Center measures AI's impact across five interconnected domains of American life — giving people a voice in each, and giving the organizations working to protect their interests the evidence to act.

Democracy
How is AI changing Americans' experience of democracy — their trust in institutions, their civic participation, and the information they rely on to govern themselves?
  • AI and the information environment: misinformation, media trust, ability to distinguish reliable sources
  • Civic trust and confidence in democratic institutions in an AI era
  • Citizen voice: do people feel they have any say in how AI is governed?
  • AI and elections: impact on voter information, participation, and confidence
Education
Is AI expanding access to knowledge and serving all students — or widening gaps and creating new dependencies?
  • Student experience with AI learning tools — engagement, agency, critical thinking
  • Teacher and educator perspectives: help or burden?
  • Parent trust in AI-mediated education
  • Equity: is AI closing access gaps or widening them?
  • Career readiness in an AI-transformed economy
Humanities & Culture
How is AI reshaping the cultural and information landscape — and do artists, creators, and the public have adequate protections?
  • Public attitudes toward AI-generated art, writing, and media
  • Artist and creator protections: awareness, support, and perceived fairness
  • Trust in media and ability to identify AI-produced content
  • AI's impact on journalism, news quality, and the information ecosystem
Labor & Economy
Is AI enhancing human work or replacing it? Do workers have agency, voice, and dignity in how AI is deployed?
  • Purpose, meaning, and satisfaction as AI reshapes roles
  • Worker agency and voice in AI deployment decisions
  • Manager support — the 8.7× multiplier on whether AI empowers or threatens
  • Skills, economic mobility, and pathways to better work
  • Frontline and blue collar workers — most affected, least studied
Security
Do Americans feel that AI is making them safer — or more surveilled? What standards do they want?
  • Public perceptions of AI in national security and cybersecurity
  • AI-powered surveillance and personal privacy concerns
  • Community safety: is AI strengthening or undermining it?
  • Attitudes toward autonomous weapons and military AI
  • What safeguards and transparency people expect
How It Works

A Continuous Research Cycle That Compounds Over Time

The Center runs on a quarterly cycle. Each round builds on the last — producing richer data, deeper insights, and a growing public platform that ensures people's experiences stay at the center of the AI conversation.

Phase 01
Convene & Listen
A working advisory group spanning all five domains — economists, AI researchers, educators, civil rights leaders, artists, union leaders, community organizers, journalists. Expert interviews that surface the questions people are living but existing research hasn't asked. The signature partner helps shape who's at the table.
Phase 02
Build the Instrument
A rigorously developed survey with every question tested through cognitive interviews — real respondents walking through each item, explaining what they think it asks and how they'd answer. Refined until the instrument produces data that holds up to scientific scrutiny and earns public trust.
Phase 03
Field at Scale
Nationally representative data collection via the Gallup Panel — the largest probability-based panel in the country. Address-based sampling reaches every American, including those traditionally excluded: rural communities, non-English speakers, lower-income households, frontline workers, communities of color. The voices most affected by AI and least heard.
Phase 04
Go Deeper
Qualitative interviews with respondents whose data tells a compelling story. A warehouse worker managed by algorithms. A teacher watching students change. An artist whose work was used without consent. A community leader navigating AI in a rural town. Human stories that give people a face, a name, and a platform.
Phase 05
Publish & Convene
Media launches, policy briefings, community convenings, leader roundtables, monthly content. The research enters the national conversation — ensuring that people's actual experiences with AI inform the decisions being made about it. The signature partner's name on every finding.
Phase 06
Track & Learn
The full cycle repeats quarterly. Trend data accumulates. The Center gets smarter about what to ask and who to listen to. Ongoing measurement evaluates whether investments in protections, policy, and people-centered AI are translating into real outcomes in people's lives.
