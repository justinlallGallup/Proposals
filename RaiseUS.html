A Partnership Proposal · Gallup × RAISE US · July 2026

A People Strategy for the AI Economy

RAISE US has assembled the coalition, the capital and the state partnerships to help American workers make the transition to an AI economy. Gallup proposes to supply what comes next: the science to find the Americans who will build that economy, a development system for every worker making the transition, and the gold-standard evidence that proves what works.

Overview

This is an American moment. Millions of American workers hold jobs vulnerable to AI, and most are not convinced anyone is on their side: 61% of Americans believe AI will destroy more jobs than it creates, and only 2% fully trust it. Yet Americans are united on one priority: in Gallup's research with the Special Competitive Studies Project, 72% support creating or expanding AI workforce training and education programs, the single most popular policy option across party lines. The mandate exists. The deeper problem is that the evidence leaders need does not exist. The country measures what AI is doing to workers and almost nothing about what is working for them, which employers, which programs, which states are getting the transition right. Gallup believes the solution lies in four things: measurement reform, a people strategy, a highly individualized AI-upskilling treatment, and evidence of success.

What Gallup Proposes

Gallup, founded in 1935 to report the will of the people, and today the world's leading authority on the workplace, proposes a partnership with RAISE US built as two workstreams. American Builders is the strategy: Gallup science, deployed inside RAISE US programs to find the Americans most likely to build the organizations and jobs of the AI economy, and to develop every worker making the transition. The Official Statistics of the AI Economy is the proof: gold-standard measurement that makes RAISE US the most credible source of evidence on the AI economy in the country, built for the leaders RAISE US serves, the nation's governors and the chief executives of America's largest employers.

American Builders

Identifying the Americans most likely to build the AI economy's new companies and jobs, and developing every worker in transition.

What we're solving for

A coming jobs crisis. As AI reshapes work, there will not be enough good jobs to go around, unless someone builds them.

The breakthrough

The solution

A massive increase in quality jobs, created through the intentional, early identification and accelerated development of the nation's builders.

Executive Summary

Gallup proposes integrating its entrepreneur identification science at the front door of RAISE US programs: the RAISE US American Builders Inventory, a new edition of Gallup's builder instrument, built expressly for this partnership, to identify the participants with the highest natural potential to start and grow organizations: for-profit startups, nonprofits, charities, family businesses, and new ventures inside existing companies. All building creates new economic energy. A Gallup innovation called CliftonStrengths gives every participant a report on their top five strengths, aimed at the transition ahead, and at the highest-potential path to a job for them.

Early Identification

The RAISE US American Builders Inventory

America has never identified entrepreneurial talent the way it identifies athletic or academic talent, early, systematically, everywhere. Gallup analytics estimate that of the roughly four million students who graduate high school each year, some 200,000 have the talent to build organizations with no limits, and that talent appears at the same rate in low-income communities as in wealthy ones, we just don’t know which ones they are. Gallup will build RAISE US that system: the RAISE US American Builders Inventory, a new edition of Gallup's entrepreneur instrument, created expressly for this partnership and carried under the RAISE US name.

The inventory measures the ten innate talents Gallup's research shows successful builders share, the people who create economic energy where none previously existed. Every participant is scored 0–100 on each talent and overall, benchmarked against the general population and the top quartile of builders in Gallup's national database, so program teams see not only what someone's talents are, but how rare they are.

When Gallup measured CEOs from the Inc. 5000, 23% showed exceptional builder talent, against half of one percent of the general population, and the highest-talent builders are dramatically more likely to run million-dollar businesses, exceed sales and profit goals, and grow their teams: three times more likely to build large businesses and grow them significantly.

Development

CliftonStrengths, for every participant

Identification finds the builders. Development is for everyone. Every participant in a RAISE US program receives CliftonStrengths, Gallup's inventory of the 34 talent themes that describe what a person naturally does best, completed by more than 30 million people worldwide. Where the American Builders Inventory tells the program who to invest in, CliftonStrengths tells each participant who they are: a personal development language they keep for life.

It is also the engine of coaching and mentoring through the transition. Gallup's research shows people who use their strengths every day are far more engaged and more productive, and Gallup's coaching frameworks and development materials give every participating state a ready-made system for turning self-knowledge into momentum.

Component 01 · The Public Face

The National Dashboard: Gold-Standard Statistics

For GovernorsFor CEOs

A public dashboard designed to become the national reference, previewed in the example ticker just below, and built on two ledgers. The economic ledger starts with a measurement reform: reporting Working Adults and Non-Working Adults, a clearer frame than the monthly unemployment rate, which misses the Americans who are underemployed or not looking, a group approaching 20 million, alongside jobs created and businesses started, including what is being built with AI. The human ledger tracks what no government statistic captures: workforce ambition, job optimism, purpose, and how fully Americans have adopted AI in their daily work. The economic ledger draws on official government statistics, reframed and reported on Gallup's cadence; the human ledger is fielded monthly on the Gallup Panel.

The human ledger is the advantage no one else can copy, and the point. Ambition, optimism and adoption are leading indicators: they move before the economy does, while the classic economic numbers trail. Government statistics can count positions; only Gallup can measure ambition at national scale. And Gallup has built national alternatives to official labor statistics before, from daily employment tracking to its Payroll-to-Population measure, a capability this partnership revives for the AI economy.

The Gallup Panel, the engine behind the monthly polling
  • The largest probability-based panel in the U.S.: approximately 100,000 U.S. adults, surveyed continuously since 2004.
  • Scientifically recruited: members are selected through random-digit-dial and address-based sampling; no one can opt in, which keeps professional survey-takers out.
  • Weighted to national statistics: samples are weighted to U.S. Census Bureau and federal benchmarks to represent the U.S. adult population.
  • Every mode, every design: web, mail, phone and SMS administration, supporting cross-sectional, time-series and longitudinal designs.
  • Built for tracking: a controlled survey load and strong retention let Gallup re-interview the same Americans over time.
  • Quality-checked: embedded attention and verification checks on every wave.
Full detail: what the dashboard delivers
  • Measurement reform: reporting Working and Non-Working Adults alongside the headline rate, counting the discouraged, underemployed and not-looking Americans the official number misses, a group approaching 20 million.
  • Business creation tracking: including new-business formation and the businesses being started with AI.
  • Emotional economics: ambition, purpose, engagement and workers' relationship with AI, trended over time.
  • A regular release rhythm: released on a regular calendar, like GDP and the unemployment rate, with recurring data moments timed to the news cycle and full methodological transparency.
  • New U.S. official statistics: a public dashboard, embeddable ticker and media-ready briefs carrying the RAISE US partnership.
  • Trusted numbers by design: probability-based sampling and published methodology, so the statistics can be cited by anyone, argued by no one.
Component 02 · Evidence for Governors

The Governor's Scorecard: All 50 States

For Governors

States control workforce policy, the funding, the credentialing, the incentives, so nowhere does this partnership matter more than in the hands of the nation's governors. And what governors need is evidence. Gallup will field gold-standard, probability-based representative surveys in every participating state, beginning with the four launch states and built to scale to all 50, designed to report on key segments of the population and supplemented with official state data, producing state-level estimates of actual behavior, not just sentiment: who is using AI and how, who is being displaced, who is landing and keeping good jobs, and how each state's workforce compares to every other.

The deliverable is the playbook itself: a landmark annual study of 10,000 U.S. working adults, fielded on the full Gallup Panel, that identifies the employers getting responsible transitions right, a place for every worker in the AI economy, and turns their practices into guidance every CEO and CHRO can act on. Full detail: the employer research program
  • The landmark study: how AI is reshaping engagement, purpose, retention, hiring, growth and the manager's role, across industries and organization sizes.

  • Employer playbooks and toolkits: practical, thematic guidance: AI and retention, AI and hiring, AI and the manager, retrain-and-redeploy models.
  • A recurring index option: grow the study into a quarterly AI & Work measure: the jobs-report equivalent for AI's human impact.

Component 04 · On Call

Ongoing & Thematic Research

For GovernorsFor CEOs

A standing research capacity RAISE US can point at whatever question comes next. Some needs are predictable, a thematic study on AI and early careers, a state deep dive ahead of a legislative session, rapid polling before a policy moment. Others will not be. This component keeps Gallup's researchers, instruments and fielding infrastructure on call, deployable as needed across the year.
