A Partnership Proposal — Gallup × RAISE US · July 2026

A People Strategy for the AI Economy

RAISE US has assembled the coalition, the capital and the state partnerships to help American workers make the transition to an AI economy. Gallup proposes to supply what comes next: the science to find the Americans who will build that economy, a development system for every worker making the transition, and the gold-standard evidence that proves what works.

This is an American moment. By RAISE US's own estimate, 50 million American workers hold jobs vulnerable to AI — and most are not convinced anyone is on their side: 61% of Americans believe AI will destroy more jobs than it creates, and only 2% fully trust it. The deeper problem is that the evidence leaders need does not exist. The country measures what AI is doing to workers and almost nothing about what is working for them — which employers, which programs, which states are getting the transition right. RAISE US has the coalition, the capital and the state partnerships to change that. What it needs next is a people strategy, and measurable proof of what works — in hand by the time government is able to act.

What Gallup Proposes

Gallup — founded in 1935 to report the will of the people, and today the world's leading authority on the workplace — proposes a partnership with RAISE US built as two workstreams. America's Builders is the people strategy: Gallup's talent science, deployed inside RAISE US programs to find the Americans most likely to create the new companies and jobs of the AI economy, and to develop every worker making the transition. The Official Statistics of the AI Economy is the proof: gold-standard measurement that makes RAISE US the most credible source of evidence on the AI economy in the country — built for the leaders RAISE US serves, the nation's governors and the chief executives of America's largest employers.

Identification

The RAISE US America's Builders Assessment

Gallup will build RAISE US its own instrument: the RAISE US America's Builders Assessment, a new edition of Gallup's entrepreneur assessment created expressly for this partnership — the same validated science and national benchmarks, carried under the RAISE US name, with reporting configured for RAISE US program teams and deployable from a single cohort to an entire state. RAISE US does not license someone else's tool. It launches its own.

The science underneath is Gallup's definition made measurable. A builder is someone who can create economic energy where none previously existed — the people whose ventures become new products, new opportunities and, most important for this mission, new jobs. Gallup's research across tens of thousands of entrepreneurs, non-entrepreneurs and students isolated the ten innate talents successful builders share, and produced a 30-minute assessment — validated for ages 14 and up, on the same science as CliftonStrengths — that measures them. Every individual receives a score from 0 to 100 on each talent, plus an overall talent score, benchmarked against the general adult population and the top quartile of builders in Gallup's national database — so a program team sees not only what someone's talents are, but how rare they are, along with the kind of building role a person naturally plays, from revenue generation to operations to product innovation.

And the instrument separates. When Gallup assessed CEOs from the Inc. 5000 — leaders of some of America's fastest-growing companies — 23% showed exceptional builder talent, against half of one percent of the general population. Talent predicts performance: the highest-talent builders are dramatically more likely to run million-dollar businesses, to exceed sales and profit goals, to be planning to grow their teams — and three times more likely to build large businesses and grow them significantly.

Development

CliftonStrengths, for every participant

Identification finds the builders. Development is for everyone. Every participant in a RAISE US program receives CliftonStrengths — Gallup's assessment of the 34 talent themes that describe what a person naturally does best, completed by more than 30 million people worldwide. Where the America's Builders Assessment tells the program who to invest in, CliftonStrengths tells each participant who they are: a personal development language they keep for life.

It is also the engine of coaching and mentoring through the transition. Gallup's research shows people who use their strengths every day are far more engaged and more productive — and Gallup's certified coaching network and development materials give every participating state a ready-made system for turning self-knowledge into momentum.

The urgency behind this development layer sharpened just this month, in new Gallup findings featured in Fortune. AI use has crossed a threshold in the American workforce — yet almost no one says it has transformed how work gets done, and Gallup's research shows the gap closes through people, not technology: the organizations that invest in their managers and their people's development capture the gains, while the rest pour money into tools and wait.

The human ledger is the moat — and the point. Ambition, optimism and adoption are leading indicators: they move before the economy does, while the classic economic numbers trail. Government statistics can count positions; only Gallup can measure ambition at national scale. And Gallup has built national alternatives to official labor statistics before, from daily employment tracking to its Payroll-to-Population measure — a capability this partnership revives for the AI economy, and extends globally, because the competition for that economy is worldwide.

Full detail: what the dashboard delivers
  • The Working Adults reform — reporting Working and Non-Working Adults alongside the headline rate, counting the discouraged and underemployed Americans the official number misses.
  • Job and business creation tracking — including new-business formation and the ventures being started with AI.
  • The human indicators — ambition, purpose, engagement and workers' relationship with AI, trended over time.
  • A release rhythm built for attention — recurring data moments timed to the news cycle, with full methodological transparency.
  • A public destination — dashboard, embeddable ticker and media-ready briefs carrying the RAISE US partnership.
  • Trusted numbers by design — probability-based sampling and published methodology, so the statistics can be cited by anyone, argued by no one.
Component 02 · Evidence for Governors

The Governor's Scorecard: All 50 States

For Governors

States control workforce policy — the funding, the credentialing, the incentives — so nowhere does this partnership matter more than in the hands of the nation's governors. And what governors need is evidence. Gallup will field truly representative measurement in all 50 states, with sample sizes that can report on every segment of the population, producing state-level estimates of actual behavior, not just sentiment: who is using AI and how, who is being displaced, who is landing and keeping good jobs, and how each state's workforce compares to every other.

It begins where RAISE US already is — Arkansas, Connecticut, Maryland and Utah — and scales to a scorecard for every governor in the country, on identical instruments so the comparisons are real. No other research organization can field this.

Full detail: the state evidence base
  • Launch-state baselines first — Arkansas, Connecticut, Maryland and Utah measured in year one, before pilots scale.
  • State-by-state benchmark reports — each governor sees their workforce against the nation and against peer states.
  • Actual-behavior measures — AI use, displacement, retraining uptake and job outcomes, not just attitudes.
  • Every segment reportable — sample designs sized to report by age, industry, education and community type within each state.
  • One instrument, fifty states — identical questions everywhere, so a governor's number means the same thing in Annapolis and Little Rock.
  • Policy-lab-ready data — clean, documented state datasets delivered for RAISE US's research and policy work.

Component 04 · Measurement & Impact

Proof of What Works: Independent Program Measurement

For GovernorsFor CEOs

RAISE US's operating model is deliberate: pilot, measure, scale what works — and let the evidence make the case that governments will fund. That model only functions if the measurement is independent and beyond dispute. Gallup proposes to serve as that independent measurement arm: baselining every pilot before launch, measuring against RAISE US's own definition of success — whether workers land and keep good jobs — and delivering the evidence the policy lab needs, on the timeline Gina set: measurable proof by the time government is able to act.

Because the measurement is Gallup's, the results carry weight with the people RAISE US most needs to move — a legislature weighing funding, a governor weighing expansion, a CEO weighing adoption. The experiments are RAISE US's. The audit is Gallup's. That separation is what makes the proof credible.

Full detail: the evaluation system
  • Baseline before launch — every pilot measured before it begins, so impact is attributable rather than asserted.
  • A common outcome framework — the same success measures across every pilot and state, so results can be compared and scaled.
  • Rigorous designs — pre/post and comparison-group evaluation matched to each pilot's structure.
  • The human outcomes too — wellbeing, purpose and confidence measured alongside employment, because a good transition is more than a paycheck.
  • Policy-ready evidence briefs — findings packaged for governors, legislatures and funders, built to move decisions.
  • The independent auditor role — Gallup's independence is the credential; the proof is only as strong as the neutrality of whoever measures it.
