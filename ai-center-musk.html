The Problem

Everyone Has an AI Narrative. Nobody Has Independent Data.

OpenAI funds the OpenAI Foundation. Google funds Google.org's AI research. Anthropic funds Anthropic-aligned studies. Microsoft funds Microsoft-affiliated measurement. Every major AI company is now funding research about its own impact — and the findings reliably conclude that AI is going great.

Meanwhile, the public isn't buying it. Only 2% of Americans fully trust AI. 61% expect it to destroy more jobs than it creates. 79% want safety rules even if it slows development. And 89% of executives report no measurable AI effect on productivity despite billions in investment.

The gap between what AI companies say is happening and what people are actually experiencing is growing. And there is no independent, credible, nationally representative measurement system that can tell you which side is right. That's the gap Gallup is filling.

The Center

Gallup Is Building an Independent AI Research Center. One Partner Gets to Shape It.

The Gallup Center for AI & American Life will be an ongoing, nationally representative research program measuring what AI is actually doing to the way Americans work, get information, learn, access services, and live their daily lives. No spin. No narrative. Just data — collected from the largest probability-based panel in the country, reaching every American including those that online polls and convenience samples systematically miss.

Gallup is committing its 80-year measurement infrastructure and its reputation for independence. We are seeking one signature partner to co-shape the research agenda and the public platform. The partner doesn't just fund research — they own the credibility that comes from backing the only independent, ongoing measurement of AI's real-world impact.

The right partner is someone who doesn't need the data to come out a certain way. Someone who wants to know what's actually true — and is willing to put their name on it regardless of what the numbers say.

What the Partner Shapes

  • The research agenda — which questions get asked, which populations get oversampled, which findings get elevated
  • The public platform — quarterly data releases, national media, policy briefings, all carrying the partner's name alongside Gallup's
  • The advisory group — who's at the table, from economists to engineers to the people AI is actually affecting
  • The accountability function — ongoing measurement of whether AI investments, products, and policies are delivering what they promise
  • Global reach — extending the measurement to 155+ countries through the Gallup World Poll, the only existing infrastructure for this at global scale

What It Measures

Four Things Nobody Is Independently Tracking

AI companies publish usage numbers. Economists model GDP impact. Consulting firms survey their own clients. None of them are independently measuring what AI is doing to the actual daily lives of ordinary people across the country. That's what the Center does.

Work & the Economy
Is AI actually making work better — or is that just what the companies selling it say?
  • Is work more fulfilling or less? Do people have more autonomy or less?
  • The 8.7× manager variable — the biggest predictor of AI success that nobody's talking about
  • Are people gaining skills and moving up, or being deskilled and stuck?
  • Where is the AI-generated value actually going — workers, shareholders, or consumers?
  • Frontline workers: the people AI affects most and studies least
Information & the Public Square
Is AI making the information environment better or worse? Can people tell what's real?
  • Trust in information sources — including AI-generated content
  • Can people distinguish AI-produced content from human-produced content?
  • AI and misinformation: is the problem growing or overstated?
  • AI as news source: only 1.7% use it for news. Why? What would change that?
  • The media and platform trust landscape — measured independently
Education & Human Capital
Is AI making people smarter and more capable — or more dependent?
  • Student experience with AI: engagement up or down?
  • Are people developing new skills through AI or outsourcing the thinking?
  • Teacher and educator reality vs. the edtech sales pitch
  • Career readiness: is AI preparing the next generation or leaving them dependent?
Government & Institutions
Is AI making government and public services work better — or just adding another layer of waste?
  • Is government AI deployment delivering better services to citizens?
  • Are public institutions using AI efficiently or wasting resources?
  • Do people trust institutions more or less as AI mediates more of the relationship?
  • AI and civic participation: is it helping or eroding democracy?
How It Works

Quarterly Cycle. Continuous Data. Compounding Value.

Not a one-time study. A continuous measurement engine that produces independent data every 90 days — getting sharper over time as trend data accumulates and the questions get better.

Phase 01
Assemble the Right People
A working advisory group of engineers, economists, workers, small business owners, and researchers. Not a ceremonial board. People who know what questions matter because they're living the answers. The signature partner helps shape who's at the table.
Phase 02
Build the Instrument
Rigorously developed survey questions tested through cognitive interviews with real people. Not questions designed to get a particular answer. Questions designed to find out what's actually true. Refined until the data holds up to any scrutiny.
Phase 03
Ask Everyone
Nationally representative data collection via the Gallup Panel — the largest probability-based panel in the country. Not an online convenience sample that over-represents people who spend time filling out surveys. Address-based sampling that reaches every American — rural, urban, connected, disconnected, every income level, every background.
Phase 04
Get the Real Stories
In-depth interviews with people whose data tells a story. Not handpicked testimonials. Respondents selected because the data flagged something interesting, unexpected, or important. The unfiltered texture behind the numbers.
Phase 05
Put It in the Open
Media releases, policy briefings, monthly content, and an annual report — all public, all carrying the signature partner's name, all independent. The data enters the conversation and everyone has to deal with it, whether it confirms their narrative or not.
Phase 06
Track What Changes
The cycle repeats quarterly. Trend data shows what's getting better and what's getting worse. Before-and-after measurement tells you whether specific AI products, policies, and investments are delivering what they promise — or not.

Gallup has been independently measuring what Americans think, experience, and live for over 80 years. No AI product to sell. No narrative to protect. No political agenda to advance. Just data — nationally representative, scientifically rigorous, and trusted precisely because it's independent.

The Center extends this infrastructure into the AI era. The measurement systems exist. The panel exists. The global reach exists. The reputation exists. What's needed is the partner who wants to put their name on the independent record.
