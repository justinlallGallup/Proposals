GALLUP × SNAP
Proposed Research Partnership

Democracy, Technology
& the Next Generation

Gallup proposes a research partnership with Snap to produce independent, credible, and publicly influential research on the intersection of social media, civic engagement, and youth well-being — positioning Snap as a leader in supporting the work that matters most to the next generation.

Explore the engagement options ↓

A gap exists in the national conversation. There is no annual, population-level study that measures the full arc of young people's democratic experience — from whether they feel heard, to whether they know how to participate, to whether they believe participation matters. Pew and CIRCLE do important work on youth voting and registration, but nothing at this scale examines how technology, community, and family shape civic habits across an entire generation.

Gallup has the data to change that. Our Democracy for All study — conducted in partnership with the Kettering Foundation — has already surveyed 20,000+ Americans on civic engagement, institutional trust, community involvement, and democratic commitment. Within this dataset, the Snap-specific findings are striking.

Key finding: Snapchat users show meaningfully higher civic engagement than non-Snapchat social media users across efficacy, community involvement, and belief in citizen power. These differences persist — and in most cases widen — when comparing only heavy social media users (3+ hours per day), suggesting something specific to the platform rather than overall usage levels.

The community connection numbers are particularly noteworthy given that community involvement is the strongest predictor of democratic commitment in the broader dataset. Meanwhile, Gallup's parallel research on parenting and adolescent well-being shows a decades-long decline in parental rule-setting that correlates directly with higher screen time and poorer youth mental health — a story with enormous public salience that Snap can help tell responsibly.

What we're proposing is a set of research options — ranging from a pro bono analysis Gallup would conduct independently, to funded studies using existing data, to comprehensive new research programs — all authored by Gallup with full editorial independence.

Research Directions
Three Lines of Inquiry

Each addresses a distinct dimension of the same question: how are technology, community, and family shaping the civic and emotional lives of the next generation?

📊

Platform & Civic Life

How does civic engagement differ across social media platforms? What is it about Snapchat's architecture — close friends, visual communication, ephemerality — that correlates with stronger community connection?

🗳️

Youth & Democracy

What drives young Americans to engage — or disengage — from civic life? Where are the barriers? What role does technology play in shaping civic habits, and where is education and community infrastructure making a difference?

🏠

Parenting & Screens

How are American families navigating digital life? What parenting approaches actually correlate with better adolescent mental health? And where do parents need more support in an era of constant connectivity?

Phased Approach
From Data to Narrative to Impact

Each engagement option follows the same structure — quantitative research at the core, with optional qualitative depth and communications amplification.

01

Quantitative Research

Survey design, Gallup Panel data collection, weighting, analysis, and a comprehensive report with data visualizations and a Gallup.com feature article.

02

Qualitative Depth

Key informant interviews with domain experts, and in-depth interviews with participants to surface the human stories behind the data.

03

Communications & Activation

Launch events, media pitching, satellite media tours, and convening support to ensure findings reach policymakers, media, and the public.
