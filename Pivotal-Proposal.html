Research Framework

Health Shapes Power — At Home, at Work, and Beyond

This research will generate new findings that help make the case for investing in women’s health by quantifying how health issues — symptoms overlooked, needs unmet, care delayed, lives affected — shape women’s ability to make decisions, control resources, and shape policies and perspectives.

💼

Workplace & Economic Power

What share of women say health issues — perimenopause, menopause, reproductive health, pregnancy, postpartum, mental health — have caused them to step back at work, miss days, forgo a promotion, or decide against starting a business?

  • Work missed and days quantified by health condition
  • Career decisions deferred or abandoned
  • Entrepreneurial ambitions shelved due to health
🏛

Civic & Community Power

How do health conditions drive women to step back from community leadership, volunteerism, activism, and serving in school or civic roles?

  • Community engagement and leadership attrition
  • Volunteerism and activism participation
  • Stepping back from personal passions and hobbies
🏠

Family & Personal Power

How do women’s health challenges affect quality time with children and families, ability to care for loved ones, and overall wellbeing?

  • Quality time with kids and family reduced
  • Caregiving capacity diminished by health burden
  • Mental health impacts of perimenopause, postpartum, and midlife
Survey Content

What We’ll Measure

A ~15 minute survey instrument designed to generate headline-ready findings while capturing baseline data on public sentiment around women’s health. Topics will be refined collaboratively during instrument development.

Lived Experiences & Impact on Power

  • Impact of perimenopause/menopause, reproductive health, pregnancy/postpartum, and mental health on specific life decisions
  • Work missed + quantified days by condition
  • Decisions against career changes, promotions, or starting a business
  • Stepping back from community leadership, volunteerism, activism
  • Reduced quality time with children and families
  • Mental health experiences connected to perimenopause/menopause and pregnancy/postpartum

Healthcare Access & Public Sentiment

  • Autonomy over health decisions
  • Pain minimized, misunderstood, or dismissed by healthcare professionals
  • Access to healthcare and concern about losing access
  • Experience seeking care for menopause symptoms, contraception, mental health
  • Use of AI platforms for mental health support
  • Most trusted source for health information (doctor, social media, AI, friends/family)
  • Health decisions influenced by social media content
  • Public support for prioritizing women’s health — by government, philanthropy, and private sector
  • Support for women making their own health decisions
  • Belief that women deserve whole-person care (mental + physical)
Audience segmentation: The survey will include Pivotal’s typing tool questions so that the data can be segmented at the analysis stage accordingly. Pivotal will receive crosstabulations, a deidentified dataset inclusive of the weighting variable for future analysis. Gallup will also obtain permission from respondents to share their email address with Pivotal/StatSocial for the purposes of further internal analysis at Pivotal.
Phased Approach

From Instrument to Impact

Designed for a September 2026 release. Each phase builds on the last to move from data to narrative to public conversation.

01

Survey Design & Data Collection

Collaborative instrument development, cognitive testing, programming, fielding via the Gallup Panel (nationally representative), statistical weighting, and data processing. Gallup’s iterative process ensures the survey is designed for both analytic rigor and media-ready findings.

02

Quantitative Analysis & Qualitative Interviews

Full quantitative analysis mapped to the health-power framework, plus optional qualitative interviews that surface the human stories behind the data — making the findings concrete, specific, and impossible to dismiss.

03

Communications & Media Activation

Co-branded reporting, strategic media pitching, potential paid media partnership, and coordinated launch activities designed to bring attention, energy, and support for critically underfunded areas of women’s health.

Communications Support

Designed for Coverage from Day One

Gallup’s strategic communications team doesn’t treat media as an afterthought — research is designed with coverage in mind from the first question. Our team connects partners to leading journalists and influencers, leveraging longstanding relationships with top-tier outlets.

Media Partnership & Pitching

Gallup will work with Pivotal’s communications team to develop a strategic media plan designed to maximize coverage of the findings. This includes:

Pre-release: Identifying and pitching exclusive or embargoed access to a top-tier outlet (e.g., New York Times, Washington Post, CNN) to create a tentpole moment. Gallup has existing relationships across broadcast, print, and digital media and can broker new ones where needed.

Paid media partnership: Support for Pivotal’s planned paid editorial partnership, where the outlet creates a content package pairing data with human storytelling. Gallup helps design data points for editorial impact and provides supporting materials.

Post-release: Broader media pitching to drive a second wave of coverage — op-eds, broadcast segments, podcast features. Qualitative interview footage (if produced) provides broadcast-ready human stories to pair with data.

Reporting & Content

Standard Report (10 pages): Executive summary, key findings with data visualizations, and topline implications. Designed as a concise, shareable document for media, policymakers, and stakeholders.

Expanded Report (30 pages): Comprehensive deep-dive including full methodology, detailed cross-tabulations by demographic group, thematic analysis, policy implications, and narrative context from qualitative findings (if conducted). Designed as the definitive reference document.

Gallup.com Article: A companion piece published on Gallup.com reaching Gallup’s global audience of policymakers, business leaders, and media professionals.

Satellite Media Tour: Coordinated broadcast appearances across major U.S. outlets to amplify findings and put a face on the data through Gallup analysts and/or Pivotal spokespeople.

Women’s Health Partnership

Hologic Global Women’s Health Index

In partnership with Hologic, Gallup created the world’s most comprehensive global survey documenting women’s health across 142+ countries. Now in its fifth year, the Index has become a critical accountability tool, with elements integrated into the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation’s Global Gender Gap Report. Hologic credits the Index with boosting employee engagement and providing a platform for executives to engage with national decision makers on women’s health investment.

Policy & Global Governance

Driving Legislation & Institutional Action

Gallup’s ILO Women at Work study — the first-ever global account of attitudes toward women and work across 142 countries — provided data that ILO country teams used in ministerial briefings to advocate for the adoption of Convention 190 against workplace violence and harassment. Gallup’s FINDEX data became the official G-20 Financial Inclusion Indicator and tracks the World Bank’s Universal Financial Access Goal, informing financial inclusion strategies in 110+ countries.
