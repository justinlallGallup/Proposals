The Questions

The Debates That Need Better Data — and What Gallup Would Track

The Debate
Is AI actually boosting productivity — or are the margin gains just from layoffs and cost-cutting?
"I bet you dollars to donuts it's not AI." — All-In, May 2026
What Gallup tracks: Whether employees report doing more, better work with AI — or whether their experience is just fewer colleagues and more tasks. The human side of the productivity question that earnings calls will never show you.
The Debate
Is AI displacing workers or creating new opportunities?
Vanguard says AI-exposed jobs grew faster. The public says 61% expect net job loss. Which is right?
What Gallup tracks: Both sides — at the individual level. Are specific workers gaining skills and moving up? Are others being deskilled and stuck? The same national data, tracked quarterly, resolves the debate with evidence instead of estimates.
The Debate
Do companies have 500 days to prove AI ROI — and will they?
"You can't will profits to go up. Anheuser-Busch has to eventually sell more beer."
What Gallup tracks: The worker-level experience inside companies deploying AI. Not what the CEO claims in the earnings call — what the employees actually report about whether AI is changing their work, their output, and their engagement.
The Debate
Why have Americans turned on AI — and does it matter?
All-In, April 2026: "Anti-human perception" of AI among the public
What Gallup tracks: Trust, attitudes, and lived experience — continuously. Not a snapshot of opinion, but an ongoing record of how the public's relationship with AI evolves as adoption accelerates. And whether the trust gap is a perception problem or an experience problem.
The Debate Nobody Has Data For
What is AI actually doing to the quality of people's work and lives?
Not GDP. Not earnings. Not labor market abstractions. Whether work provides meaning. Whether people feel they have agency. Whether the gains are reaching frontline workers or concentrating at the top. Whether communities are thriving or struggling. The experience data that doesn't exist — and that Gallup is building.

How It Works

Quarterly Cycle. Continuous Data. Compounding Over Time.

Each cycle builds on the last. Trend data accumulates. The debates get sharper because the evidence gets better.

Phase 01
Assemble the Right People
A working advisory group — economists, engineers, AI founders, workers, small business owners. People who know what to measure because they're living the transition or investing in it. The signature partner helps shape who's at the table.
Phase 02
Build the Instrument
Rigorously developed survey questions tested through cognitive interviews with real respondents. Not designed to confirm a thesis. Designed to find out what's happening. Refined until the data holds up under the brightest spotlight.
Phase 03
Ask Everyone
Nationally representative data via the Gallup Panel — the largest probability-based panel in the country. Address-based sampling that reaches every American, including populations that online surveys systematically miss. The full picture, not a skewed sample.
Phase 04
Get the Real Stories
In-depth interviews with respondents whose data tells a story. A factory worker. A nurse. A small business owner. The human evidence that makes data relatable and findings impossible to ignore.
Phase 05
Release & Distribute
Signature partner gets early access. Then: media launches, policy briefings, investor sessions, monthly content, annual report. The data enters the national conversation — and the partner's name is on it.
Phase 06
Track What Changes
The cycle repeats quarterly. Trend data builds. The big questions — productivity, displacement, ROI, human impact — get clearer every 90 days. The debates evolve because the evidence does.
