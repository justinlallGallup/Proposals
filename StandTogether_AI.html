01 — The Crisis

The Workplace Is Breaking — and AI Isn't Fixing It

Employee engagement has fallen to its lowest level since 2020. In the U.S., just 31% of workers are engaged — an 11-year low. Globally, the number is 20%. The cost: $10 trillion in lost productivity, roughly 9% of global GDP.

Meanwhile, organizations have poured an estimated $40 billion into enterprise AI. The returns? An NBER survey of nearly 6,000 global executives found 89% report no measurable effect on labor productivity. In Gallup's own data, only 12% of employees in AI-implemented organizations say AI has transformed how work gets done.

03 — The Foundation

Power of Purpose Proved the Model.

In 2025, Gallup and Stand Together released The Power of Purpose — a nationally representative study of 4,475 U.S. working adults that introduced the Work Purpose Index: a three-item measure of whether employees believe their work contributes to something important, benefits others, and provides daily meaning.

The findings were unambiguous:

Engaged at work
50%
vs. low-purpose employees
9%

13%

Employees with strong purpose are 5.6× more engaged, far less burned out, and significantly less likely to be looking for new jobs. And 30% of workers say their ideal job would give them a sense of purpose — on par with compensation.

But Power of Purpose was fielded before AI began reshaping how work gets done at scale. The urgent question now: What happens to purpose, engagement, and retention as AI enters the workplace? Does AI amplify purpose — or erode it? Can employers harness AI to make work more meaningful, not less? Nobody is measuring this. We should be.

04 — The Reframe

The AI Narrative Is Dominated by Fear.
Nobody Is Studying What's Right.

The public conversation about AI and work has been captured by a single storyline: jobs will be destroyed, workers will be displaced, the future is uncertain. Gallup's own SCSP research confirms this: 61% of Americans believe AI will destroy more jobs than it creates. Only 2% fully trust AI.

But this is only half the story — and it's the half that everyone is already telling. What's missing is equally important: Where is AI making work better? Where are employees becoming more engaged, more productive, more purposeful because of AI — and what are those organizations doing differently?

06 — The Study

AI and the Future of Work:
A Landmark Employer Study

A comprehensive, nationally representative study of the American workforce designed to answer the questions employers need answered most — and that nobody else is measuring: How is AI actually reshaping engagement, purpose, retention, hiring, and growth? What separates organizations getting human gains from AI from those getting nothing?

AI & Engagement

Is AI boosting or eroding engagement? Does it depend on how it's introduced — top-down mandate vs. manager-supported adoption? What engagement elements (Q12) are most sensitive to AI integration? Where are the bright spots?

AI & Purpose

Extending the Work Purpose Index into the AI era. Does AI make work feel more or less meaningful? When AI automates routine tasks, do employees feel freed to do higher-value work — or disconnected from the work itself?

AI & Retention

How does AI adoption affect intent to stay? Are organizations losing talent because of how they're deploying AI — or retaining it because AI makes the job better? What role does transparency play?

AI & Growth

Is AI expanding opportunities for employee development — or narrowing them? Are employees learning new skills through AI, or is AI replacing the tasks that taught them? How do strengths-based organizations differ?

AI & Hiring

How is AI changing who gets hired and how? Are skills-first approaches accelerating? What do workers think of AI-mediated recruiting, screening, and onboarding?

AI & the Manager

The critical node. Gallup's data already shows managers are the #1 predictor of AI adoption success. This study would go deeper: What do managers need? What are the best managers doing differently? How do we equip every manager to champion AI in a strengths-based way?
