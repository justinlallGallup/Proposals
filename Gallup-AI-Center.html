The Gallup Center for AI & American Life

Gallup is establishing a dedicated research center to measure how AI is changing the way Americans work, learn, access care, and participate in their communities. We are seeking one signature partner to shape the initiative.

The Opportunity

AI Has Enormous Potential to Improve How People Work, Learn, and Live. The Evidence Base Hasn't Kept Up.

AI is already expanding what people can do — giving individuals access to expertise that was once scarce, helping employers make better decisions, enabling students to learn at their own pace, and opening new pathways to economic opportunity. The potential is extraordinary.

But we're flying blind on the most important questions. Is AI actually delivering on that potential in people's everyday lives? Where is it working well, and what makes the difference? Is it reaching the people and communities who stand to benefit most? When work changes, does it still provide meaning and purpose? When students use AI to learn, are they developing deeper capabilities?

The measurement systems society relies on — labor statistics, GDP, educational assessments — were built for a different era. They can track jobs and wages but not whether work is fulfilling. They can track school enrollment but not whether learning is deepening. They can track economic output but not whether the gains are reaching the people who need them most. What's missing is a measurement system designed for this moment.

The Center

Gallup Is Building a Dedicated Research Center. One Partner Will Shape It.

The Gallup Center for AI & American Life will be an ongoing research and public engagement program — the first continuous, nationally representative effort to measure how AI is changing the lived experiences of Americans across work, health, education, and community life.

Gallup is committing its measurement infrastructure, its scientific credibility, and its 80-year track record of giving Americans a voice on the issues that matter most. We are seeking one signature partner to co-shape the research agenda, the public platform, and the national conversation the Center will drive.

The signature partner doesn't just fund research. They shape what questions get asked, how findings reach the public, and which audiences the Center convenes. They become the name alongside Gallup on the most credible, ongoing measurement of AI's impact on American life.

If OpenAI Foundation Is the Partner

The Center's research agenda would align with the Foundation's commitment to understanding AI's economic impact, supporting workers and communities through the transition, and building long-term economic security. Specifically, the partnership could shape the Center to focus on:

  • Where AI is expanding what people can do and access — career guidance, healthcare, legal and financial expertise, education — particularly for those least served by existing systems
  • Whether AI-driven economic transitions are leading to better work, more stability, broader capabilities, and more real choices in people's lives
  • How workers experience AI deployment — including whether they have agency and voice in the process
  • When work provides meaning, purpose, and satisfaction as AI reshapes roles — and what conditions make that possible
  • Which communities and populations are benefiting from AI and which are being left behind — with the depth to understand why
  • Whether investments in worker support, retraining, and policy interventions are actually producing the outcomes they're designed for
  • Global measurement via the Gallup World Poll — tracking AI's impact in low- and middle-income countries alongside the U.S.

The Research

Four Domains. Continuous Measurement. The Full Picture.

The Center measures AI's impact across four interconnected domains of American life. The signature partner helps shape which questions are prioritized, which populations are oversampled, and which findings are elevated — but the scope ensures the Center captures the complete picture.

Work & Economic Life
How is AI changing people's experience of work — and is it expanding or narrowing economic opportunity?
  • Purpose, meaning, and satisfaction as AI reshapes roles
  • Worker agency and voice in AI deployment
  • Manager support — the 8.7× multiplier
  • Skills development and economic mobility
  • How AI-generated value is distributed
  • Frontline, blue collar, and service workers
Health & Wellbeing
Is AI expanding access to care, improving health outcomes, and reaching the people who need it most?
  • AI as equalizer — access to expertise in underserved areas
  • Trust in AI-mediated health guidance
  • Patient and provider experience
  • Mental health and wellbeing
  • Overall life wellbeing tracked against AI exposure
Education & Learning
Is AI deepening learning, expanding access, and preparing the next generation — or creating new dependencies?
  • Student experience with AI learning tools
  • Teacher and educator perspectives
  • Engagement, agency, and critical thinking
  • Parent confidence and trust
  • Career readiness in an AI-transformed economy
Community & Civic Life
Which communities are thriving through the AI transition — and what conditions make the difference?
  • Community-level economic health and opportunity
  • AI and public services — is government delivering better?
  • Civic trust and citizen voice
  • Geographic variation: rural, urban, post-industrial
  • Community organizations' capacity to support people
How It Works

A Continuous Research Cycle That Compounds Over Time

The Center runs on a quarterly cycle. Each round builds on the last — producing richer trend data, deeper insights, and a growing public platform.

Phase 01
Convene & Listen
A working advisory group of economists, AI researchers, workforce leaders, educators, healthcare experts, union leaders, small business owners, and community organizers. Expert interviews that surface the questions existing research hasn't asked. The signature partner helps shape who's at the table.
Phase 02
Build the Instrument
A rigorously developed survey with every question tested through cognitive interviews — real respondents walking through each item. Refined until the instrument produces the kind of data that holds up to scientific scrutiny and earns public trust.
Phase 03
Field at Scale
Nationally representative data collection via the Gallup Panel — the largest probability-based panel in the country. Address-based sampling reaches every American, including those hardest to reach: rural, lower-income, non-English speaking, frontline workers. Oversamples of the populations that matter most to the research agenda.
Phase 04
Go Deeper
Qualitative interviews with respondents whose data tells a compelling story. Real people, real names, real experiences. The human texture that turns findings into stories media wants to tell and the public wants to hear.
Phase 05
Publish & Convene
Media launches, policy briefings, executive roundtables, community convenings, monthly content. The research enters the national conversation — with the signature partner's name on every finding, every article, every event.
Phase 06
Track & Learn
The full cycle repeats quarterly. Trend data accumulates. The Center gets smarter about what to ask and who to listen to. Before-during-after measurement evaluates whether interventions and investments are producing real outcomes in people's lives.
