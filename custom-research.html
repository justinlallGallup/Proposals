The Gallup Panel
Probability-Based. Nationally Representative. Scientifically Defensible.
The Gallup Panel is one of the only large-scale, probability-based research panels in the United States — recruited via address-based sampling to ensure every adult has a known, nonzero probability of selection.

Why the Gallup Panel Is Different

Unlike opt-in internet panels, the Gallup Panel is built on probability-based recruitment. Members are selected through address-based sampling (ABS) from the USPS Delivery Sequence File, which covers virtually every residential address in the country. This means Gallup Panel results can be generalized to the full U.S. adult population with known margins of error — the gold standard for survey research.

The panel includes more than 100,000 active members with deep demographic, attitudinal, and behavioral profiling — enabling precise targeting of subpopulations, oversamples of hard-to-reach groups, and rapid deployment of custom surveys.

Survey Design

Every instrument is custom-built by Gallup research scientists. Includes cognitive testing, question validation, and cultural adaptation to ensure concepts measure what they are intended to measure.

Data Collection

Web-based data collection through the Gallup Panel, with options for telephone supplementation and address-based sampling to reach populations underrepresented online.

Weighting & Analysis

Multi-stage weighting using iterative proportional fitting (raking) to align sample demographics with the most current Census Bureau estimates. Full subgroup analysis and cross-tabulations.

Reporting & Delivery

Comprehensive analytical reports with executive summaries, data visualizations, and full methodological documentation. Raw datasets delivered cleaned, weighted, and documented.
