The Assessment
The CliftonStrengths Difference

A Deeper Framework for Lasting Change

Many organizations rely on behavioral assessments that categorize people into broad types. CliftonStrengths goes far deeper — it identifies 34 distinct talent themes across four leadership domains, producing over 33 million possible combinations of a person's top five themes alone. Every leader gets a profile that is truly unique, not a four-letter category.

More importantly, CliftonStrengths doesn't just describe personality — it measures how people think, feel, and behave in ways that can be productively applied. It's a development tool, not just a typing tool. The research backs this up: Gallup's data shows that individuals coached on their strengths are six times more likely to be engaged in their work and three times more likely to report an excellent quality of life.

In practice, this means moving from "I'm a certain type" to "Here's exactly how my Strategic, Responsibility, and Activator themes can help me take ownership of this initiative and drive it forward." That level of specificity is what makes the difference between an assessment that sits in a drawer and one that changes how people lead.

Team Grid Mapping: Beyond individual profiles, Gallup's Team Grid visualizes how the team's collective strengths distribute across all four domains — revealing where the team is naturally strong, where gaps exist, and how to pair complementary talents for maximum impact.
