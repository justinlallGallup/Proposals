Year 2 Research Continuation

Expanding the LOR–Gallup Partnership

Gallup is eager to continue and deepen our partnership with the LOR Foundation, building on Year 1’s research on rural community leadership to explore the conditions and experiences that make rural communities places where people see a meaningful future for themselves and their families.

In the LOR–Gallup Year 1 research, we focused on people and champions, exploring their motivations and barriers to action. For Year 2, Gallup proposes (1) repeating quality of life perception polling for its value as trending data, and (2) going deeper into rural livability. We seek to understand the conditions and experiences that make rural communities places where people see a meaningful future for themselves and their families. Practically, our goal is to uncover specific insights about the factors that rural Americans value and what is needed to deliver a thriving future for rural America — providing a clear roadmap for where and what funders can fund in rural places, and how policymakers can make funding more accessible to rural people.

Extending one of the research themes from Year 1 — perception of quality of life — rather than choosing an entirely new topic, helps build credibility with LOR’s audiences. And asking some of the same questions gives us trend data that will help attract media attention. This topic can produce tangible and unique insights for research, policy, philanthropic, and media audiences. By measuring perceptions (what people experience), aspirations (what people want), and behaviors (what people do), the research can point funders and policymakers to specific actions they can take to improve livability.

Research Framework
Three Themes of Rural Livability
Together these themes capture current conditions, future aspirations, and the pathways between them — positioning the research to identify both the drivers of current rural livability and the levers that could move rural communities from “getting by” to “thriving.”
1

Current Conditions

The current material assets, services, and social conditions of a community that explain why rural residents value their local communities.

  • Which material assets, services, and social conditions influence rural Americans’ willingness to engage, invest in, and live or remain in their local communities?
  • How are the conditions of a community that is “getting by” different from the conditions of a “thriving” community? (And how are those different from a “struggling” community?)
2

Future Aspirations

What rural Americans want their communities to look and feel like in the future, so we can specifically identify what they aspire their community to be.

  • When rural Americans talk about the future of their communities, what are their aspirations and what are their concerns?
3

Pathways to Thriving

Bridges the difference by identifying the ideas they have to make their community a “thriving” place along with the actions that would help achieve that vision.

  • When rural Americans talk about what it would take for their community to be “thriving,” what are their ideas to make that happen?
  • What actions are they willing to take to see those ideas come to life? And, what actions do they want others to take (e.g., neighbors, nonprofits, local government)?
Project Approach
A Phased, Flexible Engagement
The research is structured in three phases. Core research activities form the foundation, with qualitative and communications activities tailored to LOR’s priorities.
Phase 1 & 2 — Core Research

Survey Design, Data Collection, Analysis & Reporting

Survey instrument refresh retaining core items from the Year 1 study for trend integrity, with new items developed for Year 2 livability themes. Includes cognitive testing, data collection, weighting, analysis, a 15-page flagship report, and a Gallup.com article.

Phase 2b — Qualitative

Key Informant & Community Interviews

Key informant interviews with philanthropy, policy, and media leaders to inform survey design. In-depth community interviews conducted post-survey to bring the data to life with stories and lived experience.

Phase 3 — Communications

Amplification & Launch Strategy

Optional communications activities designed to ensure findings are seen, shared, and acted upon — from launch events and media tours to an interactive dashboard and social content production.

Phase 1 Survey Instrument Development

Survey Instrument Refresh Always Included

Core items from the Year 1 study retained for trend integrity. New items developed for Year 2 livability themes. Includes up to 10 cognitive interviews to test the instrument and full survey programming.

Phase 2 Data Collection, Analysis & Reporting
Select one data collection approach. All options include data weighting, analysis, a 15-page flagship report, and a Gallup.com article. Unlike the first survey, all options survey only rural residents. The research will be nationally representative for rural America, with options for state-specific data for LOR states.
Phase 2b Qualitative Research
Optional qualitative activities that deepen the quantitative research. Given the long development window, there is an early opportunity to engage key informants from the philanthropy, policy, and media realms to identify what they consider to be drivers of rural thriving and ensure that the tool will be actionable.

Key Informant Interviews — 20 Experts

Structured interviews with leaders in philanthropy, policy, and media conducted before survey fielding. Identifies what they consider to be drivers of rural thriving, ensures the instrument will be actionable, and begins answering Theme 3 research questions.

In-Depth Community Interviews — 30 Rural Americans

Conducted after quantitative data collection. Surfaces lived experience, local nuance, and quotable stories that bring the data to life for reports, media, and presentations. Participants can be re-contacted for media interviews with consent.

Gallup Recommends: Given the long development window before survey fielding, key informant interviews are a high-value early investment. They produce a more robust survey instrument and begin answering Theme 3 research questions. In-depth community interviews conducted post-survey add the stories and voices that drive media coverage and stakeholder engagement.

Phase 3 Communications & Amplification
All communications activities are optional. Consider approaches for creating venues and materials such as audience-specific webinars, state-based release events, or “sneak peek” social media posts about specific findings.

Gallup-Hosted Launch Event

High-visibility event at Gallup’s Washington, D.C. headquarters featuring LOR leadership, researchers, and key stakeholders. Includes keynote, data unveiling, and breakout sessions with press and funders.

Additional Gallup.com Articles & Media Pitching

Three additional Gallup.com articles highlighting specific findings, plus targeted media outreach to national and regional outlets covering rural policy, philanthropy, and community development.

Convening Support & Conference Pitching

Identification and pitching for high-impact national and regional convenings — philanthropy summits, policy forums, and rural development conferences — with speaker preparation to place LOR findings on stage.

Social Media Content Production

Professionally produced video interview with Gallup and LOR spokespeople, a Gallup podcast feature, and a suite of static and animated social media graphics for distribution across Gallup and LOR channels.

Satellite Media Tour

Coordinated satellite media tour placing Gallup and LOR spokespeople on local and regional television and radio across Mountain West markets and national outlets to expand geographic reach.

Interactive Dashboard & Mapping Tool

A fully interactive, LOR-branded online data platform featuring state-level dashboards, interactive mapping, downloadable data, and accessible research summaries — a public-facing digital home for the research.

This proposal was prepared by Gallup for the LOR Foundation. All scope and investment details are subject to final scoping discussions. Gallup is eager to continue and deepen this partnership to build on the strong momentum and impact generated in the Year 1 research.
