Why Purpose

Purpose isn't a perk. It's a performance variable.

Gallup and Stand Together's The Power of Purpose study — a nationally representative survey of 4,475 U.S. working adults — makes the business case plainly: employees with a strong sense of purpose at work are dramatically more engaged, more likely to stay, and far less likely to burn out. Yet most organizations still treat purpose as an afterthought.

Only 18% of employees say their current job has a purpose they believe in — but 30% want that from their ideal job. And just 30% of hiring leaders rate purpose as "very important." The gap between what people want and what work delivers is exactly where this Fellowship goes to work.

The Idea

Purpose is easy to declare and hard to operationalize.

Most organizations agree that purpose matters. Far fewer have figured out how to embed it where it actually moves the business — in hiring, development, engagement, and retention. The leaders who have made progress did so by committing real resources to solve a concrete problem, not because purpose sounded like a good idea.

The Power of Purpose Fellowship brings those leaders into one room. Grounded in Gallup's research and a structured discovery process, the cohort moves from diagnosis to designed, piloted interventions — each company working its own highest-stakes problem, with the group as a shared brain trust.

How It Works

A guided arc from discovery to piloted action.

Before the sprint

Discovery

Pre-program
  • We align on selection criteria together and lock a cohort of 5–8 employers across diverse industries and sectors.
  • A Jobs to Be Done discovery — led by Michael Horn, co-founder of the Clayton Christensen Institute and bestselling author of Job Moves — surfaces the real causes, not correlates, of why leaders commit resources to purpose, and what competes against it.
  • We take inventory of what's already working, inviting leaders to share success stories and current practices that drive purpose, so the cohort starts from strengths.
The core

The 12-Week Sprint

Four facilitated sessions, with applied work between
Duration
12 weeks
Cohort sessions
4 × 90 min
Cadence
~ every 3 weeks
Between sessions
2–3 hrs applied
Week1

Kickoff & alignment

Session 1 · 90 min, full cohort

Each company names what it wants from the Fellowship and its single biggest purpose breakdown. We ground the cohort in the Power of Purpose research and the discovery findings, and set shared working norms for the 12 weeks.

Before week 4, you'llPressure-test your stated breakdown against what's actually happening in your org, and gather any internal data or signals that sharpen it.
Week4

Map where purpose erodes

Session 2 · 90 min, full cohort

Map the points across the employee lifecycle — onboarding, performance, development, mobility, manager transitions — where purpose breaks down, and pick the one or two highest-leverage moments to act on. Peers stress-test each other's diagnosis.

Before week 7, you'llValidate your highest-leverage moment with your own team and confirm you have the authority and resources to move on it.
Week7

Design the intervention

Session 3 · 90 min, full cohort + paired work

Design a specific, doable intervention that accelerates work already underway — not a net-new program. This is where complementary pairing pays off: leaders who've solved a given problem coach those still working it, and vice versa.

Before week 10, you'llStand up your pilot, define how you'll know it's working, and capture an early read so there's something real to bring back.
Week10

Pilot, measure & commit forward

Session 4 · 90 min, full cohort

Share early proof points, troubleshoot with the cohort, and commit to the next phase of the pilot. We close by capturing each company's story and contribution to Gallup's cross-cohort findings.

Weeks 10–12Continue the pilot, finalize your case study, and prepare to share results at the Summit.

What You Commit To

This is a working cohort, not a webinar.

The value comes from leaders doing real work in their own organizations and being candid with one another about what's working and what isn't. Joining means committing to four things.

I.

Show up to all four sessions

Attend each 90-minute cohort session over the 12 weeks — personally, as the decision-maker, not via a delegate.

II.

Pilot inside your own org

Put a real intervention into practice between sessions and define, up front, how you'll know whether it's working.

III.

Share openly with peers

Bring your real challenges, data, and lessons to the table — and coach other leaders where your experience can fill their gaps.

IV.

Contribute to the findings

Help turn your work into a case study and a story to share at the Summit, feeding Gallup's cross-cohort findings — published research your organization helped shape.

Realistic time commitment: roughly 6 hours of cohort sessions plus 2–3 hours of applied work between each — about 12–15 hours of your time across the full 12 weeks.
